Let's Begin (Warm Up)

What do you see in the picture?

Answer: In the picture, we see the snow-covered peak of Mount Everest, the highest mountain peak in the world.

Which is the highest mountain peak in the world?

Answer: Mount Everest is the highest mountain peak in the world.

Where is it located?

Answer: It is located on the border between Nepal and China (Tibet) in Asia.

Activity 1

What happened on May 29, 1953? Follow the sequence and describe:

  • 4:00 a.m. → Hillary and Norgay awoke and got ready for their climb. Hillary discovered that his boots had frozen and spent two hours defrosting them.
  • 6:30 a.m. → The two men left camp to start their climb to the summit.
  • 11:30 a.m. → Hillary and Norgay reached the summit of Mount Everest, achieving the historic feat.
  • 11:45 a.m. → Their 15 minutes at the top were up, and they began their journey back down the mountain.

Activity 3

(a) Who was the leader of the British Everest expedition of 1953?

Answer: Colonel John Hunt was the leader of the British Everest expedition of 1953.

(b) How many climbers were chosen for the expedition?

Answer: Eleven climbers were chosen as part of the expedition team.

(c) How many climbers got the chance to climb to the top?

Answer: Only four climbers got the chance to make an attempt to reach the summit.

(d) Who were the members of the first team?

Answer: Tom Bourdillon and Charles Evans were the members of the first team.
