Activity 2

(a) Was India independent when Nazrul was born?

Answer: No, India was not independent when Nazrul was born; the country was still under British rule.

(b) Name some of the folk plays that young Nazrul had written.

Answer: Some of the folk plays written by young Nazrul are 'Daata Karna' and 'Kabi Kalidas'.

(c) Who was Nazrul's teacher?

Answer: The revolutionary fighter Nibaran Chandra Ghatak was Nazrul's teacher.

(d) Who was Kumud Ranjan Mullick?

Answer: Kumud Ranjan Mullick was a respected poet and the Head Master of the school that Nazrul joined later.

Activity 6

(a) Who inspired Nazrul to write poems?

Answer: Kumud Ranjan Mullick, Rabindranath Tagore, and Persian poets like Hafez and Khaiyyam inspired Nazrul to write poems.

(b) Why did Nazrul have to leave the army?

Answer: Nazrul had to leave the army because the 49th Bengal Regiment, in which he served, was disbanded in 1920 after the First World War.

(c) In which magazine was the poem Anandamayeer Agamone published?

Answer: The poem "Anandamoyeer Agamone" was published in the weekly magazine 'Dhumketu'.

(d) Why did Nazrul start fasting?

Answer: Nazrul started fasting as a protest against the oppressive rule and torture of political prisoners by the British.
