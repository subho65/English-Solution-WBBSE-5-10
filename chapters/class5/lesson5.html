Class V English (Butterfly)

Lesson 5: My School Days — Activity Answers

Let's Begin (Warm Up)

Do you love to go to school?

Answer: Yes, I love to go to school every day to learn new subjects and play with my classmates.

Which subject do you like the most?

Answer: I like English the most because I enjoy reading stories and learning new words.

Can you guess whose school days we are going to read about?

Answer: We are going to read about the childhood school memories of the legendary Indian filmmaker Satyajit Ray.

Activity 1

(a) To which school was the author admitted?

Answer: The author was admitted to Ballygunge Government High School.

(b) What was the school hall used for?

Answer: The school hall was used for hosting the annual prize distribution ceremony and organizing feasts on the occasion of Saraswati Puja.

(c) What was the name of the Shakespearean play that was performed in the hall?

Answer: The Shakespearean play performed in the school hall was "The Merchant of Venice".

(d) Name the two foreign actors mentioned by the author.

Answer: The two foreign actors mentioned by the author are Greenberg and Salim.

Activity 7

(a) How did B.D. Roy teach the pronunciation of 'the'?

Answer: B.D. Roy taught that the pronunciation of 'the' depends on whether the following word begins with a vowel or a consonant.

(b) How did the author get to be Ashubabu's favourite?

Answer: The author became Ashubabu's favourite because he had a talent for drawing very well.

(c) What was 'Music drawing'?

Answer: 'Music drawing' was an event where one student sang a song while another tried to capture the song's essence by drawing on the blackboard.

(d) Why did everything seem much smaller to the author when he went back to school after ten years?

Answer: Everything seemed much smaller to the author because he had grown to a height of six and a half feet, whereas he was only five feet three inches when he left school.

(e) What is 'a pleasure' according to the author?

Answer: According to the author, it is a true pleasure to look back and relive the sweet, joyful moments of school life.
