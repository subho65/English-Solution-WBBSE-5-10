Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did Jagadish Chandra Bose receive his early education in a Vernacular school?

Answer: Jagadish Chandra Bose received his early education in a Vernacular school because his father believed that one must know one’s mother tongue before learning any other language, and should also know one's own people.

(b) What did Bose’s mother do when he brought his friends home from school?

Answer: When Bose brought his playmates—who were the sons of a Muslim attendant and a fisherman—home from school, his mother welcomed them warmly and fed them all without any discrimination.

(c) Who was Lafont? How did he influence Bose?

Answer: Father Eugene Lafont was a Jesuit Father who played a significant role in developing Bose's deep interest in natural science at St. Xavier's College.

(d) Why did Bose refuse to accept his salary?

Answer: Bose refused to accept his salary as a sign of protest against the Director of Public Instruction, who offered him a much lower salary than his European colleagues despite his equal qualifications.

Activity 8

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) ‘Bose had invented several sensitive instruments’. Name a ‘sensitive instrument’ invented by J.C. Bose. What is its use?

Answer: A sensitive instrument invented by J.C. Bose is the Crescograph. It is used to measure the growth rate of plants.

(b) What did Bose prove by his experiments on plants?

Answer: By his experiments, Bose proved that plants behave and react in the same manner as human beings do, showing sensitivity to heat, cold, light, noise, and other external stimuli.

(c) Do you think Bose was uninterested about securing patency right? Why?

Answer: Yes, Bose was uninterested in securing patent rights because he believed in making his inventions public in order to allow others to advance research further, rather than seeking commercial benefit.

(d) What were the various awards and honours conferred on Bose?

Answer: The various awards and honors conferred on Bose include Knighthood in 1917, being elected as a 'Fellow of the Royal Society', and being appointed as a 'Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire'.

(e) Name some of his books and publications.

Answer: Some of his famous books and publications are 'Response in the Living and the Non-living' (1902), 'The Nervous Mechanism of Plants' (1928), and 'Niruddesher Khoje' (1896).

    Write a short biography of Prafulla Chandra Roy in about 80 words using the hints on page 97:

    Acharya Prafulla Chandra Roy, the eminent Indian chemist and educator, was born in 1861 in Khulna, which is now in Bangladesh. He started his education at a village pathsala and later attended Hare School and Albert School. In 1879, he passed his Entrance examination. After completing his F.A. from Metropolitan College in 1882, he won the Gilchrist scholarship and went to London. He earned his B.Sc. and D.Sc. degrees from Edinburgh University. Later, he served as a Professor at Presidency College, where in 1889, he was the first to prepare Mercurous Nitrite in the laboratory. He established Bengal Chemicals in 1901 and later joined Science College as Palit Professor. This great scientist passed away in 1944.
