Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What kind of a life does the vagabond want?

Answer: The vagabond wants a free, simple life lived in close contact with nature, with the open sky above him and the road beneath his feet.

(b) What are the things that do not interest the vagabond?

Answer: The things that do not interest the vagabond are wealth, hope, love, and a close friend.

(c) What would the vagabond do when winter falls?

Answer: When winter falls, the vagabond will remain outdoors in the frosty fields, warming himself near a fire, and refusing to yield to the cold.

(d) How does the poet describe a field in autumn?

Answer: The poet describes the field in autumn as frosty, covered with a white layer of frost that is "as white as meal," while the cold weather silences the birds on the trees.

(e) Why does the poet repeat the second stanza once again?

Answer: The poet repeats the second stanza to emphasize the vagabond's strong determination and firm resolve to live a life of freedom close to nature, regardless of any difficulties or seasons.

(f) What message does the poet want to give through the poem?

Answer: Through this poem, the poet wants to convey the message that true happiness and meaning in human life can be found by living a simple, care-free, and natural existence, free from material desires and worldly anxieties.
