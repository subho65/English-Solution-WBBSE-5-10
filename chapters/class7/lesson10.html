Class VII English (Blossoms)

Lesson 10: The Echoing Green — Activity Answers

Let's Share

Answer the warm-up questions:

  • What do you see in the picture?
    Answer: In the picture, we see a green playfield under a large tree where children are playing and old men are sitting and watching them.
  • What are the children doing?
    Answer: The children are happily playing sports (like football) on the open green ground.
  • What are the old men doing?
    Answer: The old men are sitting under the shade of an oak tree, laughing, talking, and enjoying watching the children play.

Activity 1

Choose the correct word/expression from the given alternatives:

  • (a) The sun makes the sky — (ii) happy
  • (b) The merry bells welcome the — (i) spring
  • (c) Old John has — (iii) white hair
  • (d) The old men are sitting under — (i) a tree
  • (e) In the evening the children come to the laps of their — (iv) mother

Activity 2

Complete the following sentences with information from the poem:

  • (a) The birds of the bush sing to the bells’ cheerful sound.
  • (b) Old John sits among the old folk, under the oak tree.
  • (c) The sport of the children reminds the old men of their own youth-time when they used to play on the echoing green.
  • (d) The children are tired when the sun does descend and their sports have an end.
  • (e) In the evening the sports of the children are seen no more on the darkening green.

Activity 3

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Which are the two birds mentioned in the poem?

Answer: The two birds mentioned in the poem are the skylark and the thrush.

(b) Who is sitting under the oak?

Answer: Old John with white hair is sitting under the oak tree among the other old folk.

(c) Pick out an expression from the poem to show that the old men are happy to see the children playing.

Answer: The expression is "Does laugh away care" which shows that they are happy and carefree when watching the children play.

(d) What do the children do when evening descends?

Answer: When evening descends, the tired children stop playing their sports and gather around the laps of their mothers, like birds in their nests, to rest.
