Activity 2

Join the following sentences with 'and' or 'but':

  • (a) In India summer is hot. In India winter is cold.
    Answer: In India summer is hot but winter is cold.
  • (b) A mouse is a small animal. An elephant is a large animal.
    Answer: A mouse is a small animal but an elephant is a large animal.
  • (c) I have a pen. I have a pencil.
    Answer: I have a pen and a pencil.
  • (d) The sun rises in the east. The sun sets in the west.
    Answer: The sun rises in the east but sets in the west.
  • (e) My father is tall. He is lean.
    Answer: My father is tall and lean.
  • (f) Bees are small. Wasps are big.
    Answer: Bees are small but wasps are big.
  • (g) In a cricket match we need a bat. We also need a ball.
    Answer: In a cricket match we need a bat and a ball.

Activity 9

Underline the mistakes in each line and write the correct passage:

Almost two thousand years ago a board-game was played in India. It was called Chaturanga. It was very popular among kings, warriors and scholars. Chaturanga means four limbs. It represented the four groups of the army: the warriors who fought on elephants, the cavalry, the charioteers and the infantry. From India the game went to Persia where it was called Shatranj. Some of the words we now use in chess were used in Persia. The term, 'checkmate', was earlier called 'Shah Mat' or 'the king is dead'. From Persia, the game went to Europe. The game is now called chess.

Activity 16

Write a short story in about 60 words using the given hints (The Thirsty Crow):

On a hot summer day, a thirsty crow flew around looking for water. Finally, he found a pitcher, but the water was at the very bottom, and he could not reach it. He saw some small pebbles nearby and began dropping them into the pitcher one by one. Slowly, the water level rose to the top. The clever crow happily drank the water, quenched his thirst, and flew away.
