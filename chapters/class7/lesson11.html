(1) Velan was very happy in the big house. — [ T ]

(2) The other people of the big house were not so happy. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "As far as he could see, the people in the big house too seemed to be equally at peace with life."

(3) No tenant remained in the house for more than a few months. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "A year later another tenant came, and then another, and then a third. No one remained for more than a few months."

Activity 3

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Who was Velan?

Answer: Velan was a perfectly contented and happy old gardener who looked after the garden of the big house.

(b) Did the tenants have any regard for the garden?

Answer: No, the tenants had absolutely no regard for the garden, often bringing in goats and letting children destroy it.

(c) Did the owners of the house visit the house frequently?

Answer: No, the owners of the house gradually dropped the practice of visiting the house and kept it locked up for years.

(d) How did the house acquire the reputation of a 'Ghost House'?

Answer: The house acquired the reputation of a 'Ghost House' because it remained locked, silent, and covered in wild growth for years, and no tenant stayed there for more than a few months.

Activity 6

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

1. Who bought the house?

Answer: A company bought the house to build small houses on the land.

2. Did they need a garden?

Answer: No, they did not need a garden and decided to clear every bit of the vegetation.

3. What was the order of the company?

Answer: The order of the company was to cut down all the trees, including the ancient margosa tree, and clear the area for building.

4. What was Velan’s request to the tree-cutters?

Answer: Velan's request to the tree-cutters was to stop hacking the margosa tree until he had bundled his clothes, walked away, and gone far out of hearing range of their axes.

Activity 13

Write a story with the help of the points on page 113 (The Boys and the Frogs):

Once, a colony of frogs lived peacefully in a small pond. One day, a group of mischievous boys gathered near the pond and started throwing stones into the water for fun, which seriously hurt several frogs. A brave old frog popped its head out and asked, "Why are you throwing stones at us?" The boys replied, "We are just playing." The frog sadly said, "What is play to you is death to us." The boys felt deeply sorry for their actions, realized their mistake, and immediately stopped their cruel sport.
