Activity 3

Identify which of the following statements are True and which are False. Give a supporting statement for each of your answers:

(a) The merchant had three daughters. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Once upon a time a wealthy merchant lived in a big mansion with his three daughters."

(b) The merchant lost his way on his return journey. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "While returning, he lost his way in a forest."

(c) Belle did not go to the castle on her own will. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "...but she pried it from him and willingly went to the Beast's castle."

(d) Belle became the mistress of the castle. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "The Beast received her graciously and told her, 'You are the mistress of the castle, and I am your servant.'"

Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) How did the fortune of the merchant change?

Answer: The fortune of the merchant changed when he lost all his wealth in a tempest at sea, forcing him and his daughters to move from their big mansion to a small farmhouse in a village.

(b) What were the gifts that the three daughters asked for?

Answer: The two elder daughters asked for jewels and fine dresses, while the youngest daughter, Belle, asked for a rare rose that did not grow in that part of the country.

(c) Why did the merchant go to the castle?

Answer: The merchant went to the castle because he had lost his way in a forest during a storm and was seeking food and shelter.

(d) What did the Beast demand from the merchant?

Answer: The Beast demanded that the merchant must either die for stealing his most precious possession (the rose) or send one of his daughters to stay with the Beast in the castle.

(e) Why did Belle go to the castle of the Beast?

Answer: Belle went to the castle of the Beast to save her father's life, as she felt responsible for his trouble because she was the one who had asked him to bring her a rose.

Activity 6

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(1) Why did the Beast decide to kill the merchant?

Answer: The Beast decided to kill the merchant because the merchant had entered his castle, eaten his food, slept under his roof, and then ungratefully stole a rare rose from his rose garden.

(2) How did the Beast look after Belle in his castle?

Answer: The Beast treated Belle very graciously. He gave her lavish clothing and food, had lengthy conversations with her, and provided her with invisible servants to wait on her.

(3) Why did Belle fail to find the prince of her dreams in the castle?

Answer: Belle failed to find the prince of her dreams because she did not realize that the handsome prince she dreamt of was actually the Beast himself, who was under a magic spell.

(4) What were the two things Belle carried to her father's house? Why did she take them?

Answer: Belle carried an enchanted mirror and a magic ring to her father's house. She took the mirror to see what was happening back at the Beast's castle, and the ring to return to the castle instantly whenever she wished.

(5) How did a handsome prince become an ugly beast?

Answer: A handsome prince became an ugly beast because a wicked fairy turned him into one after he refused to let her in from the rain.

Activity 13 (a)

A merchant was riding home through a forest after a long business trip, carrying a bag full of money. Suddenly, a heavy rain fell, and he got completely wet. He cursed the rain angrily. A robber appeared, pointed a gun at him, and pulled the trigger. Fortunately, the gunpowder was wet from the rain, and the gun did not fire. Activity 13 (b)

Last spring, I visited the beautiful Agri-Horticultural garden in Kolkata. It was filled with rows of colorful roses, lilies, dahlias, and orchids. The sweet, fragrant smell of the flowers filled the air. Seeing the bees humming and butterflies fluttering over the flowers brought me immense peace and joy.
