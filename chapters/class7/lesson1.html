Activity 2

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What must we do to know about the tale of this world?

Answer: To know about the tale of this world of ours, we must think of all the countries and all the peoples that have inhabited it, and not merely of one little country where we may have been born.

(b) How old is our earth?

Answer: Our earth is very, very old—millions and millions of years old.

(c) Who roamed the earth before the arrival of human beings?

Answer: Before the arrival of human beings, there were only animals roaming on the earth.

Activity 8

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Which are the things around us that tell us about the earth's early tale?

Answer: The things around us that tell us about the earth's early tale are the rocks, mountains, seas, rivers, stars, deserts, and fossils of old animals.

(b) Why does a pebble have a smooth surface?

Answer: A pebble has a smooth surface because it was rolled along at the bottom of the river, causing its rough edges and corners to wear away over time.

(c) How does a pebble become grains of sand?

Answer: When a pebble is carried on and on by the river, it becomes smaller and smaller until it finally becomes a grain of sand at the seaside.

(d) What does the author mean by "The Great Book of Nature"?

Answer: By "The Great Book of Nature," the author means that nature itself—including its mountains, rocks, stones, and rivers—acts like a massive book whose physical features tell the fascinating history of our earth.
