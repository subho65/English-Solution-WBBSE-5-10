Activity 2

Identify which of the following statements are True and which are False. Give a supporting statement for each of your answers:

(a) There was a commotion in the house when Uncle Podger undertook a job. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "You never saw such a commotion up and down a house in all your life, as when my Uncle Podger undertook to do a job."

(b) Uncle Podger did not want to hang the picture. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "Oh, you leave that to me. Don’t you, any of you, worry yourselves about that. I’ll do all that."

(c) Uncle Podger would cut himself and spring round the room searching for his handkerchief. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "He would try to save the glass and cut himself. Then he would spring round the room, looking for his handkerchief."

(d) The second thing that Uncle Podger would lose would be the hammer. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "The nail would be found at last, but by that time he would have lost the hammer."

Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Name the children of Uncle Podger.

Answer: The children of Uncle Podger mentioned in the story are Will, Tom, Charley, and a daughter who was sent out to buy nails.

(b) What did Uncle Podger ask the children to bring for him?

Answer: Uncle Podger asked the girl to buy sixpen'orth of nails, Tom to bring him the rule, Will to bring his hammer, and Charley to bring a kitchen-chair.

(c) How did Uncle Podger cut himself?

Answer: Uncle Podger cut himself with a broken piece of glass while trying to save the glass frame after dropping the picture.

(d) Why did Uncle Podger want his coat?

Answer: Uncle Podger wanted his coat because his handkerchief, which he needed to tie his bleeding finger, was in the pocket of the coat he had taken off.

(e) Where did he find his coat?

Answer: He found his coat when he finally got up from the chair and realized he had been sitting on it all along.

Activity 8

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Give two instances to show the carelessness of Uncle Podger.

Answer: Two instances of Uncle Podger's carelessness are: first, he dropped the picture and broke its glass frame, and second, he lost his coat and forgot he was sitting on it while blaming others.

(b) What did Aunt Podger say after Uncle Podger smashed his thumb?

Answer: After Uncle Podger smashed his thumb, Aunt Podger mildly observed that next time Uncle Podger was going to hammer a nail into the wall, she hoped he would let her know in time so she could go and spend a week with her mother.

(c) How did Uncle Podger react after finishing the task?

Answer: After finishing the task around midnight, Uncle Podger looked at the crooked and damaged wall with evident pride and said, "Why, some people would have had a man in to do a little thing like that!"

(d) Why was everyone tired after Uncle Podger put up the picture on the wall?

Answer: Everyone was completely exhausted because Uncle Podger made the entire household run around constantly, find lost tools, hold the light, and stand on chairs to help him for hours just to hang a single picture.
