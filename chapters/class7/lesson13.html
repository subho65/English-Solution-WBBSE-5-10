Activity 2

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What attracts a Jinn?

Answer: A Jinn is attracted by a girl's long, beautiful hair and pretty black eyes.

(b) List the activities of an annoyed Munjia.

Answer: An annoyed Munjia rushes out from his peepul tree, upsets tongas, bullock-carts, and cycles, and can even upset a bus.

(c) Why must one be careful while yawning under a peepul tree at night?

Answer: One must be careful because if they yawn without covering their mouth or snapping their fingers, the Munjia will jump down their throat and completely ruin their digestion.

(d) How did the boy in the class show that he was a Jinn?

Answer: The boy showed he was a Jinn by lazily stretching his arm about four yards across the room to fetch a book from the cupboard without moving from his seat.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did Anil and Mulia appear "pale and anxious"?

Answer: Anil and Mulia appeared "pale and anxious" because they had succeeded in frightening themselves after hearing the terrifying ghost stories told by Anil's mother.

(b) What did Anil's mother think when she saw the room empty?

Answer: When she saw the empty beds, Anil's mother thought that the ghosts had carried away Anil and Kamal.

(c) What was the effect of Kamal's singing on the author?

Answer: Kamal's soft singing made the dark room's atmosphere feel even more eerie and creepy to the author.

(d) Do you think Anil's mother believed in ghosts? Give a reason for your answer.

Answer: Yes, Anil's mother firmly believed in ghosts because she herself told many scary ghost stories (about Jinns and Munjias) to the boys and screamed and fainted when she saw the empty room, thinking the boys were taken by ghosts.

Activity 13

Write a story in which you and your two friends are forced to spend a night in an old, desolate building far away from home (within eighty words):

One evening, while returning from a trek, my two friends, Anil and Kamal, and I got lost in a thick forest as a massive storm came. Seeking shelter, we were forced to spend the night in an old, desolate, and spooky building. The rooms were cold, and the wind made creepy, howling sounds. We huddled close together in one room, keeping a small campfire burning. Although we stayed awake in fear all night, the morning sun finally brought relief, and we successfully found our way back home safely.
