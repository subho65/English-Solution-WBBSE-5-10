Class VII English (Blossoms)

Lesson 3: We are Seven — Activity Answers

Activity 1

Choose the correct answer from the given alternatives:

  • (a) The little girl whom the poet met was — (ii) eight years old.
  • (b) The girl lost — (iii) a brother and a sister.
  • (c) The distance between her house and the burial ground was only — (i) twelve steps.

Activity 2

Identify which of the following statements are True and which are False. Give a supporting statement for each of your answers:

(a) The little girl was good-looking. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Her eyes were fair, and very fair; / Her beauty made me glad."

(b) Five of her brothers and sisters were dead. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "Two of us in the churchyard lie, / My sister and my brother;"

(c) The graves were covered with green grass. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Their graves are green, they may be seen,"

Activity 3

Find words from the poem that rhyme with the given words:

  • curl : girl
  • head : dead / shed
  • air : fair / bare
  • clad : glad
  • be : sea / me / thee
  • tell : dwell
  • laid : maid
  • heaven : seven

Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Pick out the expressions that describe the appearance of the girl.

Answer: The expressions describing the appearance of the girl are: "cottage girl," "eight years old," "hair was thick with many a curl," "rustic, woodland air," "wildly clad," and her eyes "were fair, and very fair."

(b) With whom did the girl live?

Answer: The girl lived near the churchyard cottage with her mother.

(c) What does the girl say about her living brothers and sisters?

Answer: The girl says that out of her seven siblings, two live at Conway, two have gone to sea, and she lives with her mother.

(d) Why does the girl say, "We are seven"?

Answer: The girl says "We are seven" because she has a strong, innocent bond of love with her deceased siblings, and she does not believe that death has separated them from the family.

Activity 5

Make sentences with the following words:

  • dwell: Many tribal communities still dwell peacefully in the deep forests.
  • clustered: Beautiful wild flowers clustered round the old wooden fence.
  • rustic: The cottage had a simple, rustic charm that attracted many tourists.
  • grave: The soldiers placed wreaths at the grave of the departed commander.
  • maid: A young maid came to help with the household chores in the morning.

Activity 6

Make sentences with the following pairs of homonyms:

  • met: I met my old school friend at the market yesterday.
  • mate: Classmates should always help each other as a good mate.
  • hair: She has beautiful, long, and curly black hair.
  • hare: The swift hare lost the historic running race to the slow tortoise.
  • wonder: We stared in wonder at the massive and ancient pyramids of Giza.
  • wander: The lost travelers began to wander aimlessly in the thick forest.
  • two: My younger sister has two adorable pet puppies.
  • to: We are planning to go to the national museum next Sunday.
  • there: Please keep those colorful books on the table over there.
  • their: The hard-working farmers were busy harvesting their crops in the field.
