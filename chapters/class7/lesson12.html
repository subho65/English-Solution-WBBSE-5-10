Activity 2

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did Anne do when she could no longer control her curiosity?

Answer: When Anne could no longer control her curiosity, she went to the dining room to unpack her birthday presents.

(b) Which present did Anne see first? Why does she refer to it as ‘you’?

Answer: Anne saw her diary first among her birthday presents. She refers to it as 'you' because she considers the diary her dearest friend and personifies it.

(c) Who is the author of the book “Tales and Legends of Netherlands”?

Answer: Joseph Cohen is the author of the book "Tales and Legends of Netherlands".

(d) What did Anne want to do with the money that she got as birthday gift?

Answer: Anne wanted to buy the book 'The Myths of Greece and Rome' with the money she got as a birthday gift.

Activity 4

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) When did Anne have her birthday party?

Answer: Anne had her birthday party on Sunday afternoon (June 14, 1942).

(b) What is “The Light House Keeper”?

Answer: "The Light House Keeper" is a film that was shown to Anne and her friends at her birthday party.

(c) Who had been Anne’s best friend for years?

Answer: Lies Goosens and Sanne Houtman had been Anne’s best friends for years.

(d) Where did Anne meet her present best friend?

Answer: Anne met her present best friend (Jopie de Waal) at the Jewish Secondary School.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What is the saying that came back to Anne on one of her melancholy days?

Answer: The saying that came back to Anne on one of her melancholy days is "paper is more patient than man."

(b) Why does Anne consider her diary to be a dear friend?

Answer: Anne considers her diary to be a dear friend because she feels she has no "real" friend in whom she can completely confide her innermost thoughts and feelings.

(c) Where was Anne’s father appointed when they emigrated to Holland?

Answer: When they emigrated to Holland, Anne's father was appointed Managing Director of Travies N.V.

(d) Why was Anne's life filled with anxiety?

Answer: Anne's life was filled with anxiety because of Hitler's anti-Jewish laws, which directly impacted and threatened the safety of her family.

Activity 8

Identify which of the following statements are True and which are False. Give a supporting statement for each of your answers:

(a) Anne thought that no one will be interested in the unbosomings of a thirteen-year-old school girl. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "...it seems to me that neither I — nor for that matter anyone else — will be interested in the unbosomings of a thirteen-year-old school girl."

(b) In her diary Anne wanted to bring out all kinds of things that lay buried deep in her heart. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "I want to write, but more than that, I want to bring out all kinds of things that lie buried deep in my heart."

(c) Anne seemed to lack everything. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "No, I don't seem to lack anything. But it's the same with all my friends, just fun and joking..."

(d) Anne was born in Holland. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "My sister was born in 1926 in Frankfurt-on-Main... we emigrated to Holland in 1933..."

(e) Anne was admitted in the fourth form in the Jewish Secondary School. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "In 1941 I went, with my sister Margot, to the Jewish Secondary School, she in to the fourth form and I into the first."
