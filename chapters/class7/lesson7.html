Answer the warm-up questions:

  • Which animals are found in forests?
    Answer: Wild animals like tigers, wolves, Panthers, elephants, deer, and bears are commonly found in forests.
  • Which animals are carnivorous?
    Answer: Carnivorous animals include tigers, lions, wolves, Panthers, and leopards.
  • Which animals are herbivorous?
    Answer: Herbivorous animals include deer, elephants, rabbits, and giraffes.

Activity 3

Answer the following questions by referring to the text:

(a) How did Father Wolf come out of sleepiness?

Answer: Father Wolf came out of sleepiness by scratching himself, yawning, and spreading out his paws one after the other to get rid of the sleepy feeling in their tips.

(b) Why are the animals scared of the jackal?

Answer: The animals are scared of the jackal because Tabaqui, more than anyone else in the jungle, is apt to go mad, run through the forest, and bite everything in his way.

(c) What was the 'law of the jungle'?

Answer: The Law of the Jungle says that a tiger has no right to change his quarters (hunting grounds) without due warning, because it would frighten all game within ten miles.

(d) Why was Father Wolf angry on hearing the loud roar of the tiger?

Answer: Father Wolf was angry because the tiger, Sher Khan, was hunting a man, which is forbidden by the Law of the Jungle, and his loud roar would scare away all the other game (animals) that the wolves needed to hunt.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions by referring to the text:

(a) What was the 'most wonderful thing in the world'?

Answer: To Father Wolf, the most wonderful thing in the world was finding a naked, brown human baby who could just walk, looking up into his face and laughing, completely unafraid.

(b) Describe the 'brown baby' as he came to the wolves' den.

Answer: The 'brown baby' was a naked, soft, and dimpled little baby who could just walk. He got directly into the entrance of the cave and laughed in Father Wolf's face.

(c) Where were the parents of the 'man cub'?

Answer: The parents of the 'man-cub' had run away in panic when the tiger, Sher Khan, attacked their camp.

(d) Why did Sher Khan come to the den of the wolves?

Answer: Sher Khan came to the den of the wolves to demand that they hand over the man-cub, whom he claimed as his rightful prey.

(e) Who was Akela? Give a description of Akela.

Answer: Akela was the great gray lone wolf who led the entire Seeonee wolf pack by his strength and cunning. He lay out at full length on his rock during pack meetings.

(f) Why do you think Akela wanted the 'man cub' to be in the wolf family?

Answer: Akela wanted the man-cub to be in the wolf family because he believed that the cub was wise, could be trained as a helpful member of the pack, and would eventually help them hunt their common enemy, Sher Khan.

Activity 16

Write a short story in about eighty words (The Hare and the Tortoise):

A proud hare always boasted about his fast speed and teased a slow tortoise. One day, they decided to run a race. The hare ran fast and saw the tortoise far behind. Feeling overconfident, he fell asleep midway. Meanwhile, the tortoise kept walking steadily. When the hare woke up, the tortoise was already near the finish line. The hare's last leap was too late, and the tortoise won the race.
