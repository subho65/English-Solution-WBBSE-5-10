Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think that at the end of the day Ishan tells his mother that he likes history?

Answer: Ishan tells his mother that he likes history because the Sphinx magically showed him the fascinating historical wonders of ancient Egypt, such as the pyramids, mummification, and the tomb of Tutankhamun, which made history come alive and highly interesting for him.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why was Ishan’s father not able to accompany him to Egypt?

Answer: Ishan's father was not able to accompany him to Egypt because the prize Ishan won at the singing competition only sponsored a tour for two people.

(b) What does the Sphinx represent?

Answer: The Sphinx represents intelligence and strength, and its rock statue guards the pyramids.

(c) Name the biggest pyramid of Egypt.

Answer: Pharaoh Khufu's pyramid is the biggest pyramid of Egypt.

(d) Why was Ishan’s mother surprised when she returned to her son?

Answer: Ishan's mother was surprised because Ishan, who previously disliked studying history, had suddenly gained immense knowledge about the Pharaohs and Egyptian history during her brief absence.

Activity 8 (a)

Imagine one day you see a strange door in an old familiar building. Write a paragraph in about sixty words on your sudden visit to such a strange place:

One afternoon, while walking home from school, I noticed an old familiar building had a strange blue door that I had never seen before. When I touched the door, it slowly creaked open, inviting me into a different world. Inside, I found myself in a glowing, futuristic garden filled with whispering trees and flying colorful lights. The sight was absolutely magical, and I felt a mix of immense thrill and surprise.

Activity 8 (b)

Using the information given in the table on page 58, write a paragraph in about sixty words on the river Nile, the longest river of the world:

The Nile, located in Africa, is the longest river in the world, stretching over a length of 6,650 kilometers. It flows majestically into the Mediterranean Sea. The Nile was extremely important to the ancient Egyptians as it provided them with fresh water, food, easy transportation, and excellent fertile soil for farming. Major historical cities like Cairo, Port Said, and Alexandria are situated near this river.
