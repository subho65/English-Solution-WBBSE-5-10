Activity 3

Answer the following question:

How do you think Rip felt when he heard a voice from the distance calling?

Answer: When Rip heard a voice calling his name from a distance in that lonely, silent mountain, he probably felt surprised and a little bit frightened.

Activity 4

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The stranger wanted to assist Rip. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "He made signs for Rip to assist him with the load."

(b) All the strangers had beards. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "They all had beards, of various shapes and colours."

(c) Rip felt very thirsty. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Feeling thirsty, he had several drinks himself."

(d) Rip saw that his village had become smaller. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "He saw his village was now larger, with more people."

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) How did the stranger look?

Answer: The stranger was a short, old man with thick bushy hair and a beard, carrying something heavy in a keg on his back.

(b) What happened after Rip had drunk the liquid?

Answer: After Rip had drunk the liquid from the keg, his eyes gradually closed and he fell into a deep sleep.

(c) Why was Rip surprised when he came near the village?

Answer: Rip was surprised because he met many people he had never seen before, and everyone stared at him with surprise, making him feel like a complete stranger in his own home.

(d) What did Rip suddenly notice?

Answer: Rip suddenly noticed that his beard had grown a foot long while he was asleep.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What attracted the attention of the people when Rip entered the village?

Answer: The strange appearance of Rip, with his long beard and peculiar dress, attracted the attention of the people when he entered the village.

(b) What did Rip learn about Nicolas Vedder?

Answer: Rip learned that his old friend Nicolas Vedder had been dead and gone for eighteen years.

(c) Who looked exactly like Rip's previous self?

Answer: A young fellow leaning against a tree, who was actually Rip's son, looked exactly like Rip's previous self.

(d) How long did twenty years seem to Rip?

Answer: To Rip, the twenty years of sleep seemed to have been just one single night.

Activity 10 (b)

Write an imaginary conversation between you and your lazy brother on early rising:

Me: Wake up, Rohit! It is already 7:00 a.m. Early rising keeps us healthy and active.
Rohit: Let me sleep for ten more minutes. Morning sleep is so peaceful.
Me: No, if you wake up late, you will miss the fresh morning air and be late for school.
Rohit: But I feel tired in the morning.
Me: That is because you go to sleep late. If you sleep early, waking up early will be easy and refreshing.
Rohit: Okay, I will try to wake up early from tomorrow.
