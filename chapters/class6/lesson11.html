Activity 2

  • (a) The poor blind boy wants to know what the blessing of the sight is.
  • (b) The blind boy feels the difference between day and night by sleeping or playing.
  • (c) The boy often hears mournings about his hapless woe.
  • (d) The boy does not want his cheer of mind to be destroyed.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Whom do you think the blind boy is speaking to?

Answer: The blind boy is speaking to those around him who have the blessing of sight and often mourn or show pity for his visual impairment.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What would happen if the blind boy kept awake?

Answer: If the blind boy kept awake, it would be always day for him, regardless of whether it is day or night in reality.

(b) What does the blind boy bear with patience?

Answer: The blind boy bears with patience his hapless woe (the loss of his sight), which he can never enjoy.

(c) Why does the blind boy think that he is a king?

Answer: The blind boy thinks he is a king because despite his blindness, he keeps his cheer of mind intact and sings happily.

(d) Quote any two lines from the poem that suggest that the boy has a positive attitude towards life:

Answer:
"Then let not what I cannot have
My cheer of mind destroy;"

Activity 8 (a)

Complete the imaginary dialogue between Day and Night:

Day: Dear friend, I must declare I am more important than you.
Night: I don't know how you can say that.
Day: The sun rises during the day. People work and children go to school and play.
Night: Everyone takes rest and sleeps when I arrive.
Day: You bring darkness, I bring bright sunshine and make the world active.
Night: I have the light of the moon. I make the surroundings cool, you make them hot and sweaty.
Day: Indeed, that's true. We can then say that both of us are equally important.
Night: Yes. Mankind needs both day and night for a balanced life.
