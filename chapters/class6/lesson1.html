Activity 3

Answer the following question:

What do you think Bholenath's reaction be if he knew that the animal 'huddled in the dark' was actually a tiger?

Answer: If Bholenath had known that the animal huddled in the dark was actually a tiger, he would have been extremely terrified and run away immediately to save his life instead of grabbing it by its ears.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions:

(a) Why did Bholenath bring the tiger home?

Answer: Bholenath brought the tiger home because he mistook it for his lost donkey in the dark and stormy night.

(b) What made the neighbours come running out of their huts?

Answer: Hearing Bholenath's wife scream in fear upon discovering the tiger tied to the tree, the neighbours came running out of their huts.

(c) Why were the villagers relieved?

Answer: The villagers were relieved because the tiger, which was tied to the tree, had chewed through the rope and run away back into the jungle.

(d) How did the king reward Bholenath for his bravery?

Answer: The king rewarded Bholenath by making him the Commander-in-Chief of his army and giving him and his wife a huge house to live in.

Activity 6

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) Bholenath did not know how to ride a horse. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "I don't even know how to ride a horse", he told his wife helplessly.

(b) The king sent Bholenath a magnificent elephant. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: In the morning, the king sent Bholenath a magnificent stallion.

(c) The enemy soldiers were happy to see Bholenath. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: The enemy soldiers did not hear him. They were greatly scared.

(d) Bholenath defeated the enemy soldiers single-handedly. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: Everyone was amazed that he had defended his state all by himself against eight thousand enemy soldiers.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why was Bholenath full of despair?

Answer: Bholenath was full of despair because the king had made him the Commander-in-Chief of the army and entrusted him with the duty of protecting the state against eight thousand enemy soldiers, even though he did not know how to ride a horse.

(b) How did his wife help him to overcome his trouble?

Answer: His wife helped him to overcome his trouble by securely tying him to the magnificent stallion with a strong rope so that he would not fall off while riding.

(c) What made the enemy soldiers greatly scared?

Answer: The enemy soldiers were greatly scared when they saw a wild-looking man tied to a fierce stallion, waving branches excitedly, as they mistook him for some great warrior who had come single-handedly to defeat them.

(d) Do you think Bholenath could truly be called a legendary character? Give reasons for your answer.

Answer: No, Bholenath cannot truly be called a legendary character because all his acts of "bravery" were actually a series of misunderstandings and accidental events. He caught the tiger mistaking it for his donkey, and he scared the enemy soldiers because he was tied helplessly to a runaway horse.

Activity 10 (a)

Write a short paragraph in about sixty words describing your experience of being caught in a storm while returning home:

One night, while returning home from my aunt's place, the sky suddenly darkened. A wild wind began to blow, and heavy rain followed shortly. There was no one around on the lonely road, which made me feel quite scared. Clutching my umbrella tightly, I ran as fast as I could and finally reached the safety of my home, completely drenched but deeply relieved.
