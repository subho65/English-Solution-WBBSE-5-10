Class VI English (Blossoms)

Lesson 4: The Shop That Never Was — Activity Answers

Let's Share (Crossword Puzzle)

1. Read the following hints and solve the crossword:

Across:

  • (1) disappear → VANISH
  • (2) a piece of glass to see one's reflection → MIRROR

Down:

  • (3) the trick that makes things appear or disappear → MAGIC
  • (4) performance → SHOW

2. What do the words in the crossword tell you about?

Answer: The words in the crossword tell us about magic, illusions, and performances associated with a magician's show.

Activity 1

Rearrange the following sentences in the correct order and put the numbers in the given boxes:

  • (1) The shopman brought out a glass ball from his hair. — [ 4 ]
  • (2) It was a small shop, not so well-lit. — [ 2 ]
  • (3) Gip wanted to take the glass ball. — [ 5 ] (or [ 3 ])
  • (4) Gip dragged his father to the magic shop. — [ 1 ]
  • (5) Gip and his father were surprised. — [ 6 ]
  • (6) There were different kinds of magic mirrors in the shop. — [ 3 ] (or [ 5 ])

Activity 2

Fill in the blanks using words from the Help-Box:

Help-Box: scratched, keen, unexpected, tricks, crystal

Gip was keen to enter the magic shop. The shop had many crystal balls. Gip's father wanted to buy some tricks for his son. The shopman performed an unexpected action.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why did Gip find the shopman's palm blank?

Answer: Gip found the shopman's palm blank because the shopman used his magical skills to make the glass ball vanish instantly from his hand.

Activity 4

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The shopman drew out a business card from his hair. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: He drew a business card from his cheek and handed it to me.

(b) Gip found the magic box in his pocket. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: Gip discovered the box with surprise.

(c) The toy sword was a magic item. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "A Magic Toy Sword—this is a unique one!"

(d) The narrator found his full name on the magic box. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: And there it was, in brown paper, tied up and with Gip's full name and address on the paper.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions:

(a) Why was Gip surprised?

Answer: Gip was surprised because he discovered the magic box in his pocket after the shopman told him it was there.

(b) Why did the narrator fail to find out the cost of the magic items?

Answer: The narrator failed to find out the cost because the shopman did not pay any attention to his questions.

(c) What items did the shopman show to Gip?

Answer: The shopman showed Gip a unique magic toy sword, magic trains that ran without steam, and a box of toy soldiers that came alive.

(d) What was the cause of the narrator's amazement?

Answer: The narrator was amazed because the shopman swept the toy soldiers back into the box, closed the lid, and waved it to instantly wrap it in brown paper with Gip's full name and address on it.

Activity 6

Fill in the chart with information from the text:

Cause Effect
(a) The narrator thought that his son would be frightened. He cried at the shopman to take off the big drum immediately.
(b) Gip's father jumped at the shopman. He fell into utter darkness.
(c) The narrator was completely at a loss, finding no shop, door, or anything. the narrator called for a cab.
(d) The narrator realized that he had not paid for the items he had received. Gip's father went up and down Regent Street looking for the magic shop.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did the shopman do with the drum?

Answer: The shopman made Gip stand on a stool, put the big drum over him, and made him disappear.

(b) Why did the narrator think that his son would be frightened?

Answer: The narrator thought his son would be frightened because putting a giant, dark drum over a little boy might terrify him.

(c) Where did the narrator again find his son?

Answer: The narrator found his son again in Regent Street, standing a yard away from him, holding four parcels.

(d) What was the mental state of the narrator?

Answer: The narrator was at a complete loss and extremely confused because the magic shop had vanished completely.

Activity 9 (a)

Make meaningful sentences of your own with the following words:

  • prevent: The police took swift action to prevent the criminals from escaping.
  • disappeared: The sun slowly disappeared behind the dark clouds as night fell.
  • stared: The curious boy stared in absolute wonder at the massive dinosaur skeleton.
  • parcel: The postman delivered a large, wrapped parcel to our house this morning.

Activity 10 (a)

Suppose you see a strange object displayed on the window of a shop. Write a paragraph within sixty words on what you learn about this strange object:

One day, while returning home, I saw a glowing glass globe in a small shop window. I went inside and asked the shopkeeper about it. He told me that it was a magical map of the stars that showed real-time movements of the planets when touched. I felt a deep sense of wonder and excitement, wishing I could buy it and explore the night sky.

Activity 10 (b)

Write a paragraph in about sixty words on two of your wishes that you want fulfilled:

I have two deep wishes that I sincerely want to see fulfilled. My first wish is to travel around the world to see beautiful historic monuments like the Pyramids of Egypt. My second wish is to build a large library in my school so that all children can read interesting books. Fulfilling these wishes would bring me immense joy and satisfaction.
