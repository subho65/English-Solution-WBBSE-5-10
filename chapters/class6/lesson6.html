3. What is so enjoyable about kite-flying? Discuss with your partner.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

What made the little kite's paper stir at the sight of the tranquil sky?

Answer: The sight of the big kite rising boldly into the tranquil sky inspired the little kite, stirring his paper with a desire to try and fly.

Activity 4

Cause Effect
(a) The little kite looked at other things high over his head and feared he would fall if he tried to fly. The kite was afraid to fly.
(b) The little kite grew braver. He rose through the air alone.
(c) The little kite sailed side by side with the big kite, looking down at the ground below. The little kite was thrilled with pride.
(d) The kites flew very high up in the sky. The boys looked like small spots.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did the big kite tell the little kite to do?

Answer: The big kite told the little kite to try to fly, warning him that he would never learn at all if he let fear stop him.

(b) How did the little kite prepare himself for flight?

Answer: The little kite shook himself free for flight, trembled, and at first whirled in a frightened manner before growing braver and rising steadily through the air.

(c) Whom did the kites get as companions high up in the quiet air?

Answer: High up in the quiet air, the kites got only the birds and clouds as companions.

(d) "Oh, how happy I am" — Why did the little kite feel so?

Answer: The little kite felt happy because he had been brave, tried his best, and successfully learned to fly high into the sky.

Activity 8 (b)

Write a dialogue between two sisters, where the elder sister is encouraging the younger one to read out a patriotic poem in her school on Independence Day:

Riya (Elder Sister): Hello Tina! Have you chosen a poem for our school's Independence Day program?
Tina (Younger Sister): No, Riya. I am too nervous to read a patriotic poem in front of the whole school.
Riya: Don't be afraid, Tina! You have a beautiful voice. Reading a patriotic poem is a great way to show love for our country.
Tina: What if I forget the lines on stage?
Riya: I will help you practice every day until you know it perfectly. You just need to be brave and try!
Tina: Okay, Riya. Your words encourage me. I will try my best and recite the poem.
Riya: That's like my brave sister! I am proud of you.
