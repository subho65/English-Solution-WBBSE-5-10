Lesson 2: The Adventurous Clown — Activity Answers

Activity 2

Fill in the blanks with words given in the Help Box (one extra word is provided):

Help Box: exciting, grumbled, noble, toyshop, dull

Tuffy, the toy clown lived in a toyshop. He wished to do something noble. His friend, Timothy, felt that the clown always grumbled. But Tuffy thought that the adventures were always exciting.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

"Adventures are always exciting"—Why do you think Tuffy said so?

Answer: Tuffy said so because he was bored of his quiet, dull corner on the toy shelf and strongly wanted to do something exciting and noble to be praised as a hero by all the other toys.

Activity 4

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The farmer called the toy clown silly. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Now the other horse and cart will win! You silly, little clown!"

(b) Tuffy threw all the water at the farmer. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: He climbed the ladder and threw all the water in at the window.

(c) The sailor doll could not cook in peace. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Can't I cook in peace without you coming and throwing water at me?"

(d) Three goldfish were swimming in a big bowl of water. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: In it were swimming two fine goldfish.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions:

(a) What did Tuffy do when he got upset?

Answer: When Tuffy got upset after being scolded by the sailor doll, he ran out and sat down on a toy farm, wiping his tears.

(b) How did the toy clown put out the fire?

Answer: Tuffy climbed a ladder with a big bucket filled with water from the farm pond and threw all the water in at the window of the doll's house.

(c) Why was the sailor doll angry?

Answer: The sailor doll was angry because Tuffy threw water in through the window while he was peacefully cooking porridge, thereby spoiling his meal.

(d) What real adventure did Tuffy find at last?

Answer: At last, Tuffy found a real adventure when he saw a small doll drowning in a big bowl of water and successfully rescued her with a little net.

Activity 6

Fill in the chart with information from the text:

Cause Effect
(a) Tuffy caught hold of the little doll with a net and dragged her out, banging her head on the table. The little doll began to cry.
(b) The little doll complained to the policeman about Tuffy. The policeman doll locked Tuffy in a room in the police station.
(c) Timothy dropped the key to the floor of the room. Tuffy quickly unlocked the door and escaped with Timothy to the toy shelf.
(d) Tuffy realized that his adventures had gotten him into trouble and that the shelf was much safer and more peaceful. Tuffy decided never to leave the toy shelf again.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did Tuffy put the little net in the water?

Answer: Tuffy put the little net in the water to rescue the small doll, whom he believed was drowning in the bowl of water.

(b) What made the little doll think that Tuffy was not a hero?

Answer: The little doll thought Tuffy was not a hero because instead of saving her, he caught her in a nasty net, dragged her out roughly, and banged her head on the table, spoiling her fun swim.

(c) How was Tuffy released from the police station?

Answer: Tuffy was released when his friend Timothy, the toy dog, brought the key to the police station door and slid it under, allowing Tuffy to unlock it and escape.

(d) Why did Timothy blush?

Answer: Timothy blushed with shyness and happiness when Tuffy thanked him and called him the real hero of the day.

Activity 9

  • fiercely: The tiger roared fiercely in the deep jungle.
  • bump: The little boy fell down and got a small bump on his forehead.
  • drowning: The brave lifeguard jumped into the river to save the drowning man.

Activity 10 (a)

Write a paragraph in about sixty words on 'A boat journey' using the given hints:

On a beautiful moonlit night, my three friends and I went on a boat journey along a wide river. The peaceful rippling of the water against the wooden boat was soothing to hear. As we glided along, we could see a dark forest on either side of the river, which added a sense of mystery. The thrill of the journey under the starry sky made it an unforgettable adventure for all of us.

Activity 10 (b)

Suppose you spent a night with Tuffy in the toy shop. Write a short paragraph in about sixty words describing your experience:

Last night, I had the magical experience of spending a night with Tuffy in the toyshop. When the lights went out, all the toys came alive. Tuffy eagerly showed me around his favorite spots, including the farm pond and the giant slide. We played hide-and-seek behind the wooden blocks, and Tuffy promised me he would stay safely on his shelf instead of looking for dangerous adventures. It was a truly delightful night.
