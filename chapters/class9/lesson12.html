Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(i) How did the snake appear as it was moving through the grass?

Answer: As the great black snake moved through the grass, it went reeling by, with its head down, tongue flickering on the trail, and its diamond-patterned scales glazing in the sun.

(ii) What did the poet and her companions do when the snake was gone?

Answer: When the snake was gone, the poet and her companions took a deeper breath of relief, looked at each other, and went on their way.

Exercise 3

Rewrite the following sentences as directed:

  • (a) How happy we are here! (change into an assertive sentence)
    Answer: We are very happy here.
  • (b) Everybody knows the name of Tagore. (change into an interrogative sentence)
    Answer: Who does not know the name of Tagore?
  • (c) The painting is very beautiful. (change into an exclamatory sentence)
    Answer: How beautiful the painting is!
  • (d) Can we ever forget our childhood days? (change into an assertive sentence)
    Answer: We can never forget our childhood days.

Exercise 4

Rewrite the following sentences as directed:

  • (a) We are proud of our heritage. (use the noun form of ‘proud’)
    Answer: We take pride in our heritage. (or We have pride in our heritage.)
  • (b) We should have sympathy for the poor. (use the adjective form of ‘sympathy’)
    Answer: We should be sympathetic to the poor.
  • (c) His success was due to his labour. (use the verb form of ‘labour’)
    Answer: His success was due to his hard work. (or He succeeded because he laboured hard.)
  • (d) The song of the nightingale is very sweet. (use the adverb form of ‘sweet’)
    Answer: The nightingale sings very sweetly.
