Lesson 11: A Shipwrecked Sailor — Activity Answers

Exercise 1

Tick the correct answer from the given alternatives:

  • (1) Robinson Crusoe was shipwrecked in — (a) 1659
  • (2) Robinson Crusoe used canvas to make a — (c) tent
  • (3) The distance between the stakes Robinson Crusoe drove into the ground was — (d) six inches
  • (4) Crusoe saw some fair weather in the month of — (b) November
  • (5) The journey homeward with the fruit parcel took Crusoe — (c) three days

Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(1) What name did Crusoe give to the dismal island?

Answer: Crusoe called the dismal island the 'Island of Despair'.

(2) Where did he pitch the tent?

Answer: He pitched his tent on a flat green area just before a cave-like hollow on a hill's rocky wall.

(3) How did Crusoe go over the fence?

Answer: Crusoe used a short ladder to go over the fence and withdrew it after him.

(4) Why did he keep a journal?

Answer: He kept a journal to seriously describe his daily state of affairs in writing.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(1) What were the items Robinson Crusoe collected from the wrecked ship?

Answer: Crusoe collected wood, cable, string, a knife, nails, a gun, a hammock, canvas, ink, paper, money, some food, and provisions from the wrecked ship.

(2) How did Crusoe keep track of the number of days he spent on the island?

Answer: Crusoe cut a notch with his knife for every day upon a large wooden post to prevent losing his measure of time.

(3) In what way did Crusoe make himself a lamp?

Answer: Crusoe collected a little animal fat (tallow) and a small clay dish, added a wick to it, and thus made himself a lamp.

Exercise 6

Identify the following sentences as simple, complex and compound:

  • (a) They predicted that it would rain. → Complex sentence
  • (b) The weather being fine, the children went out to play. → Simple sentence
  • (c) Black ink on white paper, Ravi woke up early but he could not reach school on time. → Compound sentence
  • (d) Finishing his work, my father returned home. → Simple sentence

Activity 7

Do as directed:

  • (a) Tatar hoped to do well in the examination. (change into a complex sentence)
    Answer: Tatar hoped that he would do well in the examination.
  • (b) The Headmistress arrived and the meeting began. (change into a complex sentence)
    Answer: As soon as the Headmistress arrived, the meeting began. (or When the Headmistress arrived, the meeting began.)
  • (c) Ayesha was surprised when she heard the news. (change into a simple sentence)
    Answer: On hearing the news, Ayesha was surprised. (or Hearing the news, Ayesha was surprised.)
  • (d) You must work hard to be successful in life. (change into a compound sentence)
    Answer: You must work hard or you will not be successful in life.
