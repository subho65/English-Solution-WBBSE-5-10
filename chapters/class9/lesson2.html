Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(1) How many people got into the bus with the younger woman?

Answer: Two people (a woman and a man) got into the bus with her.

(2) What was the conductor’s order to the younger woman?

Answer: The conductor ordered her to take the little dog out of the bus.

(3) Why did the conductor stand triumphant?

Answer: He stood triumphant because the lady finally went upstairs, and he felt he had won the dispute.

(4) What is that rule which cannot be broken without danger to life and limb?

Answer: The rule of the road cannot be broken without danger to life and limb.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(1) When did the author feel that trouble was coming up?

Answer: The author felt trouble was coming up when the conductor's eye rested on the little Pekinese dog on a bitterly cold night.

(2) Mention two demands made by the passengers when the bus stopped.

Answer: The passengers demanded their fares back and insisted on calling the police to report the conductor.

(3) How are rules of guidance to be observed?

Answer: Rules of guidance should be observed in their spirit, not in the letter, with goodwill, common sense, and good temper.

Exercise 4

Underline the verb forms which show that the subject does something (Active) or circle (or note) the verb forms showing something is done to the subject (Passive):

  • (a) She has sung a song. [Active]
  • (b) A song has been sung by her. [Passive: has been sung]
  • (c) He will draw a picture. [Active]
  • (d) A picture will be drawn by him. [Passive: will be drawn]

Exercise 5

Change the voice of the following sentences:

  • (a) Nila has bought a book.
    Answer: A book has been bought by Nila.
  • (b) They will have seen the cricket match.
    Answer: The cricket match will have been seen by them.
  • (c) Bhola had seen a tiger.
    Answer: A tiger had been seen by Bhola.
  • (d) The nurse will attend the patient.
    Answer: The patient will be attended by the nurse.

Exercise 6

Change the voice of the following sentences:

  • (a) Do the sum.
    Answer: Let the sum be done.
  • (b) The poem was written by her.
    Answer: She wrote the poem.
  • (c) Open the door.
    Answer: Let the door be opened.
  • (d) The man is known to me.
    Answer: I know the man.

Exercise 7

Change the voice of the following sentences:

  • (a) They agreed to my plan.
    Answer: My plan was agreed to by them.
  • (b) My brother lost my favourite pen.
    Answer: My favourite pen was lost by my brother.
  • (c) The man is writing a letter.
    Answer: A letter is being written by the man.
  • (d) Titli is looking for her watch.
    Answer: Her watch is being looked for by Titli.

Exercise 9

Use the following flow chart on page 27 to write a paragraph within 100 words on how to prepare orange juice:

Orange juice is prepared through a simple, step-by-step process. At first, fresh oranges are bought from the market. Next, they are sorted carefully, and any rotten ones are removed to maintain quality. Thereafter, the good oranges are cleaned thoroughly with water, peeled, and put inside a juicer. The sweet juice is then extracted. Following this, appropriate amounts of sugar and preservatives are added to the juice for taste and preservation. Finally, the fresh juice is poured into clean bottles, tightly sealed, and it is ready to be sold in the market.

Exercise 10

Write a paragraph within 100 words on how you plan to take care of street dogs using the hints on page 27:

Street dogs often face terrible, cruel actions from people, such as being beaten or chased away. Since they are helpless living beings, it is our moral necessity to look after them. We can take care of these dogs by providing them with clean water, leftover food, and safe shelter near our localities during harsh weather. Moreover, we can vaccinate them against rabies and help arrange basic medical treatment if they are injured. In conclusion, treating street dogs with kindness, love, and compassion not only protects them but also builds a safe, harmonious community for everyone.
