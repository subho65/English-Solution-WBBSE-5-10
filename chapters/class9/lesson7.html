Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(1) Which one of Tom’s tooth had come loose?

Answer: One of his upper front teeth had come loose.

(2) How are Sid and Mary related to Tom?

Answer: Sid is Tom's younger brother, and Mary is Tom's cousin.

(3) Why did Tom ask Sid not to stir him?

Answer: Tom pretended that even a little movement would increase his extreme pain.

(4) Why did Tom pretend his toe was paining?

Answer: Tom pretended his toe was paining to stay home from school and go fishing.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(1) What did Tom remember hearing from a doctor?

Answer: Tom remembered hearing from a doctor that a certain illness could lay up a patient for three days and make him lose a finger.

(2) How did Aunt Polly react to the news that Tom was dying?

Answer: Aunt Polly was shocked, but she did not believe it. Her face grew pale, her lips trembled, and she quickly ran upstairs with Sid and Mary.

(3) How was Tom’s loose tooth taken out?

Answer: Aunt Polly tied one end of a silk thread to Tom's loose tooth with a loop, tied the other end to the bedpost, and pulled it, which pulled the tooth out.

Exercise 4

Do as directed:

  • (a) Rafique was running fast. (change into an interrogative sentence)
    Answer: Was Rafique not running fast? (or Was Rafique running fast?)
  • (b) The boy would never forget the exciting incident. (change into an affirmative sentence)
    Answer: The boy would always remember the exciting incident.
  • (c) We had a wonderful time last evening. (change into an exclamatory sentence)
    Answer: What a wonderful time we had last evening!
  • (d) He loves to play football. (change into an interrogative sentence)
    Answer: Doesn't he love to play football? (or Does he love to play football?)
  • (e) Rina tried all her plans. (change into a negative sentence)
    Answer: Rina left no plan untried. (or Rina did not leave any of her plans untried.)
  • (f) Shut the door, Robin. (change into an assertive sentence)
    Answer: Robin is requested to shut the door. (or You should shut the door, Robin.)

Exercise 6

Write a newspaper report within 100 words on the effect of a devastating flood in your district based on the hints on page 59:

Devastating Flood in Midnapore
—By a Staff Reporter
Midnapore, July 12, 2013: A devastating flood has hit the Midnapore district following several days of incessant heavy rainfall. The major rivers are overflowing, causing massive damage. Many trees have been uprooted, and over fifty villages are completely flooded under water. Tragically, there has been a significant loss of human and animal life, with crops destroyed. The state government immediately initiated rescue operations, deploying disaster relief teams to evacuate stranded people. Temporary relief camps have been set up to distribute dry food, clean water, and medicines to the flood victims.
