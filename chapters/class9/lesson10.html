Lesson 10: The Price of Bananas — Activity Answers

Exercise 1

Tick the correct answer from the given alternatives:

  • (1) The author was on his way from Faizabad railway station to — (c) Lucknow
  • (2) From the water pump the passengers were filling up their — (b) earthen pitchers
  • (3) The gentleman who came up to the author’s compartment looked like a — (a) businessman
  • (4) The price the vendor asked for the bananas was — (a) two annas
  • (5) The author drew the cartoon of — (d) Sethji

Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(1) Where did the young monkeys sit?

Answer: The young monkeys sat on the boughs of neem and tamarind trees.

(2) What did the monkey do to the loin cloth of the pious person?

Answer: A monkey snatched away the loin cloth of the pious person who was bathing.

(3) What did Sethji’s luggage include?

Answer: His luggage included a big steel trunk, several small baskets, and a brass jug.

(4) Why, according to the fruit vendor, were the monkeys disturbing the passengers?

Answer: According to the fruit vendor, the monkeys were disturbing passengers because they were hungry.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(1) What was the businessman wearing?

Answer: The businessman was wearing a white muslin dhoti, a delicate tunic, and an embroidered cap on his head.

(2) What did the monkey do after accepting the fruit vendor’s bargain?

Answer: After accepting the bargain, the monkey took the bananas with his right hand and released the embroidered cap with his left.

(3) How did the whole amusing episode end?

Answer: The episode ended on a bitter note when the rich businessman ungratefully refused to pay the poor vendor his two annas.

Exercise 5

Change the voice of the following sentences:

  • (a) Mr. Khan is known to me.
    Answer: I know Mr. Khan.
  • (b) Trina had composed a poem.
    Answer: A poem had been composed by Trina.
  • (c) I will always remember you.
    Answer: You will always be remembered by me.
  • (d) They have made him the President of the club.
    Answer: He has been made the President of the club by them.

  • (b) We have been living in this city for over ten years.
  • (c) I have just finished reading the book.
  • (d) The project will have been completed by June next year.

Exercise 7

Change the mode of narration in the following sentences:

  • (a) Rahul said, "Alas! Brazil has lost the match."
    Answer: Rahul exclaimed in sorrow that Brazil had lost the match.
  • (b) "Let us go for a walk," said Shabnam to Chandni.
    Answer: Shabnam proposed to Chandni that they should go for a walk.
  • (c) "Good morning, madam," said the students to the teacher.
    Answer: The students wished their teacher good morning.
  • (d) My father said to me, "Do not pluck flowers."
    Answer: My father advised (or ordered) me not to pluck flowers. (or My father forbade me to pluck flowers.)

Exercise 8

Write a story in about 100 words based on the given hints on page 92 (The Two Cats and the Monkey):

Once, two foolish cats found a piece of bread. Unable to decide who should keep it, they started fighting over it. A clever monkey saw them and offered to settle their dispute. He broke the bread into two parts, but made them of uneven size. To make them equal, the monkey bit a portion from the larger piece. Now, the other piece became larger, so the dispute continued. The cunning monkey kept biting from each piece until he ate both pieces of the bread entirely. The sad, foolish cats realized too late that their quarrel had left them with nothing.

Exercise 9

Write a paragraph in about 100 words on how your locality looks like at dawn using the hints on page 92:

I live in a peaceful residential locality in Salt Lake, Kolkata. At dawn, my neighborhood looks absolutely beautiful, serene, and fresh. The early morning sky is softly tinged with light shades of yellow and orange as the sun begins to rise. A gentle, cool breeze blows through the green trees, and sparkling dewdrops glisten like pearls on the leaves. The quiet atmosphere is filled with the melodious chirping of birds and the soft rustling of leaves. I deeply enjoy this peaceful time of day because it brings a sense of hope, calm, and positive energy before the busy, noisy day begins.
