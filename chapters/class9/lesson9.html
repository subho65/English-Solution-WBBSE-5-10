Lesson 9: The North Ship — Activity Answers

Exercise 1

Tick the correct answer from the given alternatives:

  • (1) The number of ships sailing by were — (a) three
  • (2) The wind carried the first ship to a — (c) rich country
  • (3) The second ship turned towards the — (d) east
  • (4) The decks of the third ship shone — (b) frostily
  • (5) The northern sky rose over the — (a) unfruitful sea
  • (6) The third ship was rigged for a — (a) long journey

Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(a) What did the wind do to the second ship?

Answer: The wind hunted the second ship like a wild beast, keeping it anchored in captivity so that it could not make any progress.

(b) Give any two points of difference between the journey of the third ship and the other two ships.

Answer: First, the third ship travelled wide and far to the north while the others went east and west. Second, the third ship was rigged for a long journey, unlike the first two which returned.

Exercise 4

Do as directed:

  • (a) The man was so weak that he could not walk. (rewrite using ‘too’)
    Answer: The man was too weak to walk.
  • (b) Titir is one of the most intelligent girls in the class. (rewrite using the positive degree of the underlined adjective)
    Answer: Very few girls in the class are as intelligent as Titir.
  • (c) As soon as he reached home, it started raining heavily. (change into a negative sentence)
    Answer: No sooner had he reached home than it started raining heavily.
  • (d) Why cry over spilt milk? (change into an assertive sentence)
    Answer: It is useless to cry over spilt milk.
  • (e) Blood is thicker than water. (change into an interrogative sentence)
    Answer: Is blood not thicker than water?
  • (f) He told a lie intentionally. (rewrite using the noun form of ‘intentionally’)
    Answer: He told a lie with intention.
  • (g) Run fast or you will be late. (change into a complex sentence)
    Answer: If you do not run fast, you will be late. (or Unless you run fast, you will be late.)
  • (h) The reporter made the news public. (rewrite using the verb form of ‘public’)
    Answer: The reporter publicized the news.

Exercise 5

Write a newspaper report within 100 words on the effect of a devastating flood in your district based on the hints on page 84:

Devastating Flood in Midnapore
—By a Staff Reporter
Midnapore, July 12, 2013: A devastating flood has hit the Midnapore district following several days of incessant heavy rainfall. The major rivers are overflowing, causing massive damage. Many trees have been uprooted, and over fifty villages are completely flooded under water. Tragically, there has been a significant loss of human and animal life, with crops destroyed. The state government immediately initiated rescue operations, deploying disaster relief teams to evacuate stranded people. Temporary relief camps have been set up to distribute dry food, clean water, and medicines to the flood victims.

Exercise 6

Imagine that you and your friend are sailors of two ships that have recently returned from their voyages. Write a dialogue within 100 words on the different experiences of your voyages:

Rabi: Welcome back, Amit! How was your voyage to the west?
Amit: It was wonderful, Rabi. The wind was favorable, and we reached a very rich and prosperous country safely. How about your journey to the north?
Rabi: Oh, it was extremely difficult! There was no wind, the decks shone frostily, and we had to battle an unforgiving, cold sea.
Amit: Wow, you truly proved your bravery on that long journey. I am glad we are both back safely!
