  • 5. The children came to know that the sun would come out after — (c) seven years

Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(a) How did the heavy storms affect the islands of Venus?

Answer: The heavy storms caused massive, overwhelming tidal waves to come over the islands of Venus.

(b) What did the children write about the sun?

Answer: The children wrote small stories, descriptive essays, or short poems about the sun.

(c) Where did the children play?

Answer: The children played inside the echoing tunnels of the underground city on Venus.

(d) How did the jungle in Venus look?

Answer: The sunless jungle was the color of dark rubber, grey ash, and black ink.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(a) Why were the children eager to see the sun?

Answer: The children were eager to see the sun because they had lived in rainy Venus all their lives, were only two years old when it last appeared, and had completely forgotten its warmth and color.

(b) What did the children do immediately after the rain stopped?

Answer: Immediately after the rain stopped, the door of the underground city slid back, and the children rushed out into the warm summertime, yelling and laughing.

(c) Why do you think that the children were feeling unhappy when it started raining again?

Answer: The children felt unhappy because their brief hour of sunshine had ended, and they realized they had to wait seven more years for the sun to appear again.

Exercise 4

Do as directed:

  • (a) I have a blue pen. (Change into a complex sentence)
    Answer: I have a pen which is blue in colour.
  • (b) Seeing a snake, the boy shouted. (Change into a compound sentence)
    Answer: The boy saw a snake and he shouted.
  • (c) I know a man who is very wise. (Change into a simple sentence)
    Answer: I know a very wise man.
  • (d) The girl who is dancing on the stage is my sister. (Change into a compound sentence)
    Answer: The girl is dancing on the stage and she is my sister.
  • (e) I met my friend and told her about an incident. (Change into a simple sentence)
    Answer: On meeting my friend, I told her about an incident.
  • (f) The sky was overcast but it didn't rain. (Change into a complex sentence)
    Answer: Although the sky was overcast, it did not rain.
