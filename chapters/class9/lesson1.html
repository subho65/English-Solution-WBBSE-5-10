Class IX English (Bliss)

Lesson 1: Tales of Bhola Grandpa — Activity Answers

Exercise 1

Tick the correct answer from the given alternatives:

  • 1. In the bokal tree there lived — (b) monkeys
  • 2. On the way back from the festival, Bhola Grandpa tightly held on to the two fingers of his — (c) grandson
  • (3) A gang of pirates were burying a — (a) large box
  • (4) Bhola Grandpa was returning from the — (d) weekly market
  • (5) Bhola Grandpa died at the age of — (b) ninety-five

Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(a) When did Bhola Grandpa let out a loud wail?

Answer: Bhola Grandpa let out a loud wail when he remembered his grandson and realized his fingers had slipped out.

(b) Where had the grandson found a cosy shelter?

Answer: The grandson had found a cosy, warm shelter under a cow's belly.

(c) What request did Bhola Grandpa make to the first man he saw on the mound?

Answer: He requested the first man he saw on the mound for some water to drink.

(d) How old was Bhola Grandpa’s wife when he died?

Answer: Bhola Grandpa's wife was eighty years old when he passed away.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(a) Why was no real treasure found under the sand dunes?

Answer: No real treasure was found because the incident of pirates burying a large box under the sand dunes was actually just a dream Bhola Grandpa had during his midday nap.

(b) What did the tiger do after Bhola Grandpa had climbed up the banyan tree?

Answer: The tiger roared, circled the banyan tree about a hundred times, and then settled down under a bush without taking its eyes off Bhola Grandpa.

(c) What was the reason of Bhola Grandpa’s death according to his wife?

Answer: According to his wife, the ninety-five-year-old Bhola Grandpa died simply because he must have forgotten to breathe.

Exercise 4

Change the mode of narration of the following sentences:

  • (a) Bulbuli said to his friend, "Will you come tomorrow?"
    Answer: Bulbuli asked his friend if he would go the next day. (or "...if he would come the following day.")
  • (b) Paulami says, "I am fine."
    Answer: Paulami says that she is fine.
  • (c) The teacher said to the students, "Keep quiet."
    Answer: The teacher ordered the students to keep quiet.
  • (d) My mother said to me, "May your dreams come true."
    Answer: My mother wished me that my dreams might come true.
  • (e) The students said, "Sir, please allow us to play in the field."
    Answer: The students politely requested the teacher to allow them to play in the field.

Exercise 5

Identify and replace the incorrectly used articles and prepositions in the passage:

On a (an) winter night I was aboard a boat. It was a moonlit night full of (on) stars. The boat was anchored in (up) a great river. I was thrilled to see the (a) beauty of nature.

Exercise 6

Write a dialogue within 100 words on the need to ban the use of animals like tigers and lions as circus attractions:

Raman: Hi Amit! Did you watch the circus yesterday?
Amit: No, Raman. I don't support circuses because they exploit wild animals like tigers and lions.
Raman: But their tricks look so entertaining and fun.
Amit: Behind those tricks lies severe ill-treatment and cage confinement. Wild animals belong to nature, not chains.
Raman: That's true. Using whip-lashes to train them is a criminal offense.
Amit: Exactly. We must ban wild animals in circuses to ensure they are treated humanely.

Exercise 7

Write a story within 100 words (An Unexpected Adventure):

Last night, we were returning from our school's educational excursion by bus. It was a dark, silent night journey. Suddenly, a loud thud shook the vehicle; the tyres had punctured, causing an unexpected breakdown. We were forced to wait for two long hours at a lonely, dark roadside spot. At first, we felt quite nervous, but we soon started singing songs to pass the time. Finally, the driver fixed the tyres, and we reached back home safely, tired but carrying a memorable adventure story to share.
