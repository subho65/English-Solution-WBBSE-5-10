Exercise 2

Answer the following questions within fifteen words:

(a) What was the first catch of the seagull’s older brother?

Answer: His older brother caught his first fish, a herring.

(b) Why did the seagull feel the heat?

Answer: The seagull felt the heat because he had not eaten since the previous nightfall.

(c) Why did the seagull dive at the fish?

Answer: Maddened by extreme hunger, the seagull dived at the fish in his mother's beak.

(d) What happened when the seagull soared upwards?

Answer: When the seagull soared upwards, his mother flew past him, her wings making a loud swish.

Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(1) Why was the seagull afraid when he ran forward to the brink of the ledge?

Answer: The seagull was afraid because there was a vast expanse of sea beneath the ledge, and he felt certain his wings would never support him.

(2) What were the seagull’s two brothers and sister doing on the plateau?

Answer: They were dozing, with their heads sunk into their wings, while their father was preening his white back feathers.

(3) What happened after the seagull’s feet sank into the sea?

Answer: After his feet sank, his belly touched the water, and he sank no further. He floated on it while his screaming family praised him.

Exercise 6

Write a paragraph within 100 words on the water cycle, using the flow-chart on page 78:

The water cycle is a continuous natural process. At first, water evaporates from various water bodies like oceans, rivers, and lakes due to the heat of the sun. Next, this water rises high into the sky as warm water vapour. As the vapour goes up, it cools down, gets heavier, and condenses to form clouds. When these clouds become too heavy with moisture, they cannot hold it any longer. Finally, the condensed water falls back to the earth as rain, refilling the water bodies, and the cycle repeats once again.

Exercise 7

Write a summary of the prose passage on page 78 (within 100 words):

Communication is an essential part of daily life for both humans and animals. Like humans, animals interact through both verbal and non-verbal means. For instance, birds sing to communicate with each other, while mammals use groans and grunts. Body language is another major mode of communication; beasts use gestures like raising fur or crouching to convey messages, while colorful insects like wasps warn predators of their stings through their brightly painted bodies.
