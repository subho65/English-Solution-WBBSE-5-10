Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think the poet "loves the season well?"

Answer: The poet loves the spring season well because it brings the warm sun, seed-time, and harvest, revives the drooping trees, fills the forest openings with beautiful bird songs, and displays the magnificent sunset on the hills.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why is it sweet to visit the wood during springtime?

Answer: It is sweet to visit the wood during springtime because the still forest is filled with beautiful wild spring flowers and warm, pleasant sunshine.

(b) What does winter's cold do to the tree?

Answer: Winter's cold strikes the tree to the heart and leaves it drooping and lifeless.

(c) What time of the day do you think it is when "the green slope throws its shadows in the hollows of the hills"?

Answer: It is the evening time, when the bright sunset fills the woods with light and causes the green slopes to throw long shadows in the hollows of the hills.

(d) Why do you think the poem is titled "An April Day"? Suggest an alternative title.

Answer: The poem is titled "An April Day" because April represents the peak of the spring season when nature revives. An alternative title could be "The Revival of Spring".

Activity 8 (a)

Write a paragraph in about eighty words on the changes that you see in nature when spring comes after winter:

When spring comes after the cold, harsh winter, nature undergoes a beautiful and magical transformation. The dull, bare branches of the trees are filled with fresh green leaves, and the drooping plants revive. Colorful flowers like roses, lilies, and marigolds bloom in gardens, filling the air with a sweet scent. The birds begin to sing their melodious songs, and the woodland glades are filled with life. The warm, bright sun brings hope, seed-time, and joy, driving away the gloom of winter.
