Activity 3

Answer the following question:

"But no one could give him a satisfactory answer" — Why do you think no one could give him a satisfactory answer?

Answer: No one could give him a satisfactory answer because his question ("Where did the clouds go?") was highly unusual and peculiar, as clouds are natural sky phenomena that move arbitrarily rather than lost children that can be tracked.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Which question haunted the boy?

Answer: The question that haunted the boy was: "Where did the clouds go?"

(b) What did the boy see in the middle of the night?

Answer: In the middle of the night, the boy saw dense, black clouds rumbling in the sky, accompanied by occasional flashes of lightning.

(c) How did he feel as he walked between the fields?

Answer: As he walked between the fields under the fierce heat of the sun, his body felt on fire and his throat felt extremely dry.

(d) What did he do after reaching the shelter of the tree?

Answer: After reaching the shelter of the tree, he splashed the cool water from the Persian wheel on his dusty feet, washed his hands and face, and drank his fill.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did the old man say about the place where he once lived?

Answer: The old man said that he once lived in a place where it had not rained for ten years.

(b) How did the boy feel as he reached the mud-hut?

Answer: As he reached the mud-hut, the boy felt a refreshing nip in the air and noticed that the earth was damp underfoot.

(c) What did the boy do as he stood under the jamun tree?

Answer: As he stood beneath the jamun tree, the boy let the remaining raindrops roll off the leaves and fall on his head and face.

(d) Why did the clouds shed rain in the absence of the boy?

Answer: The clouds shed rain in the boy's absence because they had travelled far in the dust and sun, gathered moisture, and finally discharged their rain, which is a natural meteorological process.

Activity 10 (a)

Write a story in about eighty words based on the given hints on page 34 (Two Friends and the Bear):

Once, two friends were passing through a dense forest when suddenly a huge black bear came toward them. One friend quickly climbed up a tall tree to safety, leaving the other friend helpless on the ground. Knowing that bears do not touch dead bodies, the second friend lay down flat on the ground and held his breath as if he were dead. The bear sniffed him, thought he was dead, and went away. The friend on the tree climbed down and asked what the bear whispered. The clever friend replied, "It told me not to trust friends who leave you in danger."

Activity 10 (b)

Suppose you have been waiting for a piece of good news. Write a paragraph in about eighty words on your experience of waiting:

Last month, I was eagerly waiting for my annual school examination results. The news I waited for was whether I had secured the first rank. While waiting, I felt extremely anxious, restless, and excited. Every hour felt like a whole day, and I kept checking the calendar. Finally, when my father came home with the report card showing I had indeed stood first, a wave of immense joy and relief swept over me, and I celebrated happily with my family.
