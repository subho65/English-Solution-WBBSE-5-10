2. Which of the birds mentioned above appears to be the closest friend to mankind? Why do you think so?

Answer: The crow appears to be the closest friend to mankind because it lives near human habitations and cleans our surroundings by eating garbage and waste.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think the swallow leaves the poet during the winter season?

Answer: The swallow leaves the poet during the winter season because it is a summer bird that cannot bear the cold, dreary frost of winter and flies away to warmer places.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What does the swallow do in summer?

Answer: In summer, when the weather is fine and warm, the swallow builds its nest in chimneys and chirps its musical notes for weeks together.

(b) How is the behaviour of a "gay young friend" similar to that of the swallow?

Answer: A "gay young friend" behaves just like a swallow because they stay with us only during our prosperous, happy times (summer) and quickly leave us when we face difficult, dreary times (winter).

(c) What type of a friend does the poet want?

Answer: The poet wants a loyal, steadfast friend like the sparrow, who stays close to him, sings in frost, and continues to love him even during times of sorrow and misfortune.

(d) Whom does the poet like—the swallow or the sparrow? Give reasons for your answer.

Answer: The poet likes the sparrow more than the swallow. This is because the swallow is a fair-weather friend who flies away in winter, whereas the sparrow is a loyal companion who stays close to man even in the coldest and most difficult days of winter.

Activity 6 (b)

Change the following sentences into indirect speech:

  • (i) The sailor said to us, "There will be a storm."
    Answer: The sailor warned us that there would be a storm.
  • (ii) The teacher said to the student, "Did you come to school yesterday?"
    Answer: The teacher asked the student if he/she had gone to school the day before.
  • (iii) The tired guest said to the host, "Please give me a glass of cold water."
    Answer: The tired guest requested the host to give him a glass of cold water.
  • (iv) Rita said, "How happy I am!"
    Answer: Rita exclaimed in joy that she was very happy.
  • (v) My grandfather said, "May you live long!"
    Answer: My grandfather wished (or prayed) that I might live long.

Activity 8 (a)

Write a short story in about eighty words based on the given hints on page 98 (The Kind Farmer and the Snake):

One cold winter morning, a kind farmer found a snake lying almost dead from the biting cold. Feeling sorry for the creature, he carefully brought it home and warmed it near his kitchen fireplace. Soon, the heat revived the snake, and it woke up. Instead of showing gratitude, the ungrateful snake tried to bite the farmer's young son. Seeing this, the angry farmer immediately chased the dangerous snake away with a stick to protect his family, realizing that some creatures cannot be trusted.

Activity 8 (b)

Suppose you have a bird that talks and plays with you. Write a paragraph in about eighty words on how you spent a day with the bird during your summer vacation:

During my last summer vacation, I spent a magical day with an incredible talking parrot named Mithu. My uncle had brought him to our house. To my amazement, Mithu could speak English fluently and even tell funny jokes. We spent the afternoon playing in our garden, where he sat on my shoulder and kept repeating my name. We ate fresh sweet mangoes together, and he kept shouting, "Very tasty!" It was a highly amusing and memorable day filled with constant laughter, and I felt as if I had made a truly unique and wonderful friend.
