Class VIII English (Blossoms)

Lesson 6: The Sea — Activity Answers

Let's Share

1. With which water body can you associate the following visual images? (sandy shores, salty water, foam, waves)

Answer: The Sea (or An Ocean)

2. If you are asked to compare the sea to an animal, to which animal would you compare it? Why?

Answer: I would compare the sea to a hungry dog. This is because, like a hungry dog, the sea rolls on the beach all day, gnaws at the rocks, and has a wild, noisy nature when the storm wind blows.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why does the sea become quiet at a certain time of the year?

Answer: The sea becomes quiet in the months of May or June because during these summer months, there are no strong winds or storms, leaving the weather calm and the sea peaceful.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What does the sea look like?

Answer: The sea looks like a giant, grey hungry dog.

(b) Who rocks in the stormy cloud?

Answer: The moon rocks in the stormy cloud.

(c) Where does the sea shake at his wet sides like a dog?

Answer: The sea shakes his wet sides over the cliffs on a stormy night.

(d) How do the grasses on the dune behave in May or June?

Answer: In May or June, the grasses on the dune remain extremely quiet and do not play their reedy tune.

Activity 8 (a)

Write a story in about eighty words using the given hints on page 69 (The King and the Waves):

Once, a proud king boasted of his absolute power, claiming he could control everything on earth. To prove this, he took his courtiers to the sea-shore. He stood before the rising waves and commanded them to move back. However, the waves ignored his command, lashed at his feet, and made him step back in fear. The humbled king realized that some forces of nature are beyond human control, and he admitted his foolishness to his courtiers.
