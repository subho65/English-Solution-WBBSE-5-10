Class VIII English (Blossoms)

Lesson 10: Tales of Childhood — Activity Answers

Let's Share

Answer the warm-up questions:

  • How would you describe yourself in five words?
    Answer: I am honest, hardworking, friendly, curious, and cheerful.
  • Tell the class about some significant memories of your childhood.
    Answer: A significant memory of my childhood is learning how to ride a bicycle with my father, and the joy I felt when he let go of the seat and I balanced on my own.

Activity 2

Complete the following sentences with information from the text:

  • (a) The narrator's grandfather was a fairly prosperous merchant who owned a store in Sarpsborg and traded in just about everything from cheese to chicken-wire.
  • (b) Harold Dahl had lost an arm when he was fourteen years old.
  • (c) A shipbroker is a person who supplies the ship with everything it needs when it comes into port—fuel and food, ropes and paint, soap and towels, hammers and nails, and thousands of other little items.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Do you think that the narrator was happy living in the imposing mansion?

Answer: Yes, the narrator was very happy living in the imposing mansion. His memories of the vast farm, the woodlands, the horses, cows, and the chickens represent a grand, lively, and joyful childhood environment.

Activity 4

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The narrator was the eldest child in the family. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "In 1920, when I was still only three, my mother's eldest child, my own sister Astri, died from appendicitis."

(b) Astri was the favourite child of the narrator's father. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "She was seven years old when she died. Astri was far and away my father's favourite."

(c) The narrator's father died of appendicitis. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "He was so overwhelmed with grief... that when he himself went down with pneumonia a month or so afterwards, he did not much care whether he lived or died."

(d) After his father's death, the narrator along with his other family members moved to Llandaff. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "She sold the big house and moved to a smaller one a few miles away in Llandaff."

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) How old was Astri when she died?

Answer: Astri was seven years old when she died.

(b) Why was pneumonia a very dangerous disease?

Answer: In those days, pneumonia was a very dangerous disease because there was no penicillin or other modern miracle antibiotic cures to treat it easily.

(c) What made the narrator's father reluctant to fight against the disease?

Answer: The father's deep, overwhelming grief over the sudden death of his favorite daughter, Astri, made him lose his will to live and left him reluctant to fight against his own illness.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What was the name of the narrator's first school?

Answer: The name of the narrator's first school was Elmtree House.

(b) Which blurred memory does the narrator have of his early days?

Answer: The narrator has a blurred memory of sitting on the school stairs and trying over and over again to tie one of his shoelaces.

(c) How did the narrator enjoy his journeys to and from school?

Answer: The narrator enjoyed his journeys by racing at enormous speeds down the middle of the road on his tricycle alongside his eldest sister.

(d) 'It's not much, but it's all there is left' — Why, according to you, did the narrator make this comment?

Answer: The narrator made this comment because after sixty-two years, almost all his other childhood memories of his first school had faded away, leaving only these few exciting moments of his tricycle journeys.

  • (i) She sings a song. [Active]
  • (ii) A song is sung by her. [Passive]
  • (iii) He drew a picture. [Active]
  • (iv) A picture was drawn by him. [Passive]

Activity 9

Make meaningful sentences with the given pairs of words. Use one pair for one sentence:

  • met, shop: I met my friend yesterday inside the local sweet shop.
  • remember, days: I always fondly remember the happy days of my early childhood.
  • moved, place: Our family recently moved to a beautiful new place in the countryside.

Activity 10 (a)

Write an autobiography of a broken bicycle in about eighty words:

I am an old, broken bicycle now lying silently in a dark corner of a garage. I was born in a factory and was once bright red and shiny. My happiest days began when a kind boy named Rohit bought me as his birthday gift. He rode me every day, and we raced through the neighborhood streets together. Unfortunately, one day we had a bad accident, and my front wheel and frame got badly bent. Now, covered in rust and dust, I fondly remember those glorious days of speed and laughter.
