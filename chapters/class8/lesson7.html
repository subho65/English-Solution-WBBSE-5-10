Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think Merlin and Sir Ulfius entrusted Sir Ector with the baby of the dead king and queen?

Answer: Merlin and Sir Ulfius entrusted Sir Ector with the royal baby because Sir Ector was a highly loyal, noble, and trustworthy knight, and they knew the baby would be raised in complete safety away from the lawless barons fighting for the throne.

Complete the following sentences with information from the text:

  • (b) The magnificent sword was fixed in a large stone in a churchyard.
  • (c) Arthur was eighteen when he succeeded in lifting the sword for the first time on Christmas day.
  • (d) The beautiful arm that rose from the water held a sword set with rubies and emeralds.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What was written upon the stone on which the sword was fixed?

Answer: The golden letters inscribed on the stone read: "whoever pulls out this sword is by right of birth, the king of England."

(b) What happened on the bright May morning?

Answer: On a bright May morning, a great trial took place in the presence of many people where Arthur easily lifted the sword in front of everyone and was hailed as king by the crowd.

(c) Why did Arthur need a new sword?

Answer: Arthur needed a new sword because he had lost his royal sword in a battle.

Activity 6

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The people of England were unhappy during Arthur's rule. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "King Arthur could gift peace and prosperity to England."

(b) Merlin took King Arthur to the gilded pavilion on his birthday. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "On the wedding day, Merlin took him to a richly gilded pavilion, painted crimson and dark blue."

(c) Fifty people could sit at the Round Table. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "...capable of seating fifty people."

(d) The goodness and the kindness of the knights were known all over the country. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "The stories of their goodness and kindness spread throughout the country."

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Who, according to Merlin, could possess the Excalibur?

Answer: According to Merlin, only an honest and fearless knight who protects the common people can possess the Excalibur.

(b) Describe the pavilion where the round table was placed.

Answer: The round table was placed inside a richly gilded pavilion painted crimson and dark blue, with a beautifully marbled floor.

(c) What did King Arthur's devoted knights promise?

Answer: King Arthur's devoted knights promised to help the helpless, be gentle to the weak, and punish the wicked.

(d) Why did King Arthur smile when he was informed that the Excalibur was taken back into the lake by a beautiful arm?

Answer: King Arthur smiled because he knew that the magical sword had returned safely to its rightful place in the depths of the enchanted lake, as its purpose of protecting the kingdom was fulfilled.

Activity 10 (a)

Imagine you are Merlin, the wise counsellor to King Arthur. Write a paragraph in about eighty words on the advices you would have given the king in his times of trouble:

"My dear King Arthur, in this hour of deep trouble, remember that a true king's strength lies not in his sword, but in his virtue. Do not let the greed of the rebellious barons shake your resolve. Trust only those honest and fearless knights who are devoted to protecting the weak and punishing the wicked. Be gentle to your subjects, govern with justice, and have faith in the magic of the Excalibur. Lead your people with a righteous heart, and peace shall once again reign over Britain."

Activity 10 (b)

Write a summary of the passage on page 79 (Ali and his oil jars):

Before going on a pilgrimage, a businessman named Ali entrusted his neighbor, Abdul, with a jar containing five hundred gold coins buried under olives. Sometime later, Abdul greedily discovered the coins, stole them, and filled the jar back with fresh olives. When Ali returned and found his money missing, Abdul denied everything. Fortunately, the wise Caliph Haroon-ul-Rashid proved Abdul's guilt by pointing out that the olives in the jar were fresh, which was impossible if they had been kept there for seven months. Abdul was caught and Ali got his coins back.
