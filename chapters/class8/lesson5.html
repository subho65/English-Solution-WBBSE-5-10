Activity 3

Answer the following question:

How do you think Princess September spent her days when the bird was away?

Answer: When the bird was away, Princess September spent her days feeling extremely uneasy, worried, and anxious, fearing that the little bird might forget her or take a fancy to someone else.

  • (c) Princess September told the bird he was better off in the cage because he had a beautiful golden cage and nothing to worry about from morning till night.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) 'So he suspected nothing'. Who is 'he'? Why did he not suspect anything?

Answer: 'He' refers to the little bird. He suspected nothing because he was used to Princess September holding him, and he thought she was just putting him in the cage to keep him safe from her mother's cats.

(b) Why did the little bird stop in the middle of his song?

Answer: The little bird stopped in the middle of his song because he felt unhappy and suffocated inside the cage, realizing he could not sing unless he was free.

(c) How did he try to free himself from the cage?

Answer: He tried to free himself by slipping through the bars of the cage and beating against the closed door, but he could not open it.

Activity 6

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The little bird was happy to see the rice fields and the lake from within the cage. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "The rice-fields and the lake and the willow trees look quite different when you see them through the bars of a cage."

(b) When the bird did not eat a thing, Princess September grew anxious. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "So she brought him home again and gave him supper. But he wouldn't eat a thing... The Princess was anxious."

(c) The next morning Princess found the bird hopping around the cage. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "She gave a startled cry, for there the little bird lay with his eyes closed, and he looked as if he were dead."

(d) The bird was granted freedom. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "'Then take your freedom,' the Princess said. 'I shut you in a golden cage because I loved you...'"

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) How did Princess September try to make the little bird happy?

Answer: Princess September tried to make the little bird happy by offering him a golden cage, giving him delicious supper, and taking him out for a walk to see the trees, lake, and rice fields.

(b) Why did the Princess give "a sob of relief"?

Answer: The Princess gave "a sob of relief" because when she held the motionless bird in her hand, she felt his tiny heart beating again, realizing he was not dead.

(c) Why was the little bird granted freedom by the Princess?

Answer: The little bird was granted freedom because the Princess realized that caging him would kill him, and she loved him enough to value his happiness and life over her own possessiveness.

(d) “Then he opened his wings and flew right away into the blue.” Would the little bird return to the Princess again? Give reasons for your answer.

Answer: Yes, the little bird would return to the Princess because he loved her. He promised her that he would always return, sing his loveliest songs for her, and never forget her, which he did by flying back through her open window.

Activity 10 (a)

Write an imaginary conversation between you and a caged monkey longing for its freedom:

Me: Hello little monkey! Why do you look so sad and quiet in those chains?
Monkey: Hello! I am sad because I want my freedom. I hate being chained and made to perform tricks.
Me: But the crowd enjoys your tricks so much.
Monkey: They only see my tricks, not my pain. I miss my home in the green forest, swinging from tree to tree with my family.
Me: I feel really sorry for you. No animal should be kept in chains. I wish I could unlock them.
Monkey: Thank you for understanding my feelings. Even your kind words bring me some comfort.

Activity 10 (b)

Suppose you are forbidden to act according to your own will. Write a paragraph in about eighty words describing your feelings in this situation:

Being forbidden to act according to my own will makes me feel extremely suffocated, helpless, and unhappy, much like a bird locked inside a golden cage. Freedom is the most precious thing for any living being. When my choices and freedom are completely taken away, I lose all my joy, enthusiasm, and creativity. No matter how comfortable the surroundings are, nothing can replace the happiness of living freely, expressing myself, and making my own decisions.
