Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why does the Swallow think that the statue of the Happy Prince has ‘a golden heart’?

Answer: The Swallow thinks that the statue of the Happy Prince has 'a golden heart' because although the prince is made of metal, he weeps for the sufferings and miseries of the poor people in his city and is willing to give away his precious jewels to help them.

Activity 4

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) The Swallow didn’t want to help the Happy Prince. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "I feel sorry looking at you. I don't say no to people. I will help you."

(b) The Happy Prince wanted to leave for Egypt. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "My feet are fixed here. I cannot move."

(c) The Happy Prince asked the Swallow to take a sapphire from one of his eyes to the playwright. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "He is writing a play for the theatre... take one of my sapphire eyes and give it to him."

(d) The girl in the Town Square had no shoes. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "I see a poor little girl in the town square. She has no shoes."

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did the Swallow agree to help the Happy Prince?

Answer: The Swallow agreed to help the Happy Prince because he felt deeply sorry seeing how sad the Prince was about the people's sufferings, and he did not want to say no to him.

(b) Why did the Swallow feel warm?

Answer: The Swallow felt warm on a cold night because he had done something exceptionally good to help someone in need.

(c) What made the playwright say, ‘Now I can finish my play’?

Answer: The playwright said so because he found a precious, valuable sapphire lying on his table, which he knew he could sell to buy food and firewood to easily complete his play.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What new law did the Mayor propose?

Answer: The Mayor proposed a new law stating that no bird can die in the town square, or near the statue of the Happy Prince.

(b) Why did the Mayor and the Town Councillors think the statue of the Happy Prince was not beautiful any more?

Answer: They thought the statue was no longer beautiful because it had lost its bright red ruby, its precious sapphire eyes, and all of its shining gold leaf, leaving it looking like an ugly grey beggar.

(c) Where was the Happy Prince taken?

Answer: The statue of the Happy Prince was pulled down and taken to the furnace to be melted.

(d) Why did the Mayor and the Councillors want statues of themselves?

Answer: They wanted statues of themselves because of their pride, vanity, and desire to show off their own importance to the townspeople.

Activity 8 (b)

Change the following sentences from Direct to Indirect Speech:

  • (i) The Swallow said to the statue, "I am staying with you."
    Answer: The Swallow told the statue that he was staying with him.
  • (ii) My mother said to me, "Please wait for me here."
    Answer: My mother requested me to wait for her there.
  • (iii) Raja said, "Oh! How cold the wind is!"
    Answer: Raja exclaimed in surprise that the wind was very cold.
  • (iv) The old man said to the little girl, "May God bless you!"
    Answer: The old man wished the little girl that God might bless her.
  • (v) The man said to him, "Go away."
    Answer: The man ordered him to go away.

Activity 10 (b)

Recently you gave up your seat in a crowded bus to an old, ailing person. Write a letter in about eighty words to your friend describing how you felt after helping the person:

77/2, Park Street,

Dear Amit,
I hope you are doing well. I want to share a heartwarming experience I had yesterday while travelling in a crowded local bus. The bus was packed with passengers, and I was luckily sitting on a comfortable seat. At the next stop, an old, ailing man carrying heavy bags boarded the bus and struggled to stand in the crowd. Seeing his helpless condition, I immediately got up and offered him my seat. He smiled warmly, sat down, and blessed me with a sincere heart. Although I had to stand for the rest of my journey, his grateful smile filled me with a deep sense of peace, happiness, and satisfaction. It made me realize the true joy of helping others.
