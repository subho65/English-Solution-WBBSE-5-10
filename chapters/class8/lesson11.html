Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think Mortimer never read beyond page fifty of the book?

Answer: Mortimer never read beyond page fifty of the book because the illustration on page fifty of the single man on the dark, empty platform was highly terrifying to him, and he felt a strange, unknown threat whenever he looked at it.

Activity 4

Complete the following sentences with information from the text:

  • (a) Mortimer noticed in the lamp light a dark and solitary figure he knew.
  • (b) The solitary figure faced the black mouth of a tunnel.
  • (c) Walking towards the figure Mortimer was shocked because he was staring into his own face.
  • (d) The shadowy figure stood with a candle.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) When had Mortimer seen the 'dark and solitary figure' in his childhood?

Answer: Mortimer had seen the 'dark and solitary figure' in his childhood as an illustration on page fifty of his battered old book.

(b) “Mortimer was shocked” - When was Mortimer shocked and why?

Answer: Mortimer was shocked when he walked towards the solitary figure on the empty platform, looked into its face, and realized he was staring directly into his own face.

(c) What was "steadily gaining" on Mortimer as he stumbled out of the platform?

Answer: The echoing sound of his own footsteps was steadily gaining on Mortimer as he stumbled out of the platform.

Activity 6

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) Mortimer found the old battered book on the armchair. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "By the armchair was a small, oak table on which lay a battered old book bound in red leather."

(b) Mortimer realized that the book was the same one he had read in his childhood. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "With a shock he realized it was the same book from his childhood, the book which contained the story of 'Midnight Express'."

(c) Mortimer felt immensely afraid of the things happening around him. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "Mortimer was greatly afraid. He turned the pages with trembling hands."

(d) In the flickering candlelight, the host was seen standing by the table. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "The shadowy figure stood before Mortimer. Slowly he edged forward."

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) To where did Mortimer follow the shadowy figure?

Answer: Mortimer followed the shadowy figure up the creaking stairs and into an upper room where a bright fire was burning.

(b) Why do you think Mortimer's hands trembled when he turned the pages of the book?

Answer: Mortimer's hands trembled with extreme fear and dread because he realized that the mysterious, terrifying cycle of events from his childhood book was happening to him in real life.

(c) “Mortimer tried to grasp the strange cycle of events he was going through” - what was the strange cycle of events?

Answer: The strange cycle of events was that the childhood story book he read became his real-world experience, and he found himself as both the character reading the book in the armchair and the terrifying stranger standing in front of him.

(d) Do you think the strange incidents that happened to Mortimer would not have occurred if he had not read the book?

Answer: Yes, it is highly likely that the events would not have occurred. Reading the book at age twelve planted a deep psychological fear in his mind, creating a terrifying illusion or nightmare that eventually manifested in his consciousness.

Activity 10 (a)

Suppose you find yourself alone in a railway station at night. Write a paragraph in about eighty words describing your experience:

Last winter, I had to wait alone at a quiet railway station past midnight after my train was delayed. The platform was completely empty, lit only by a dim, yellow lamp. A cold wind whistled through the pillars, and the distant sound of insects made the silence feel spooky. Every small rustle of dry leaves made me jump with fear. When the distant headlight of my train finally appeared in the darkness, a massive wave of relief washed over me. It was a highly tense and memorable night.
