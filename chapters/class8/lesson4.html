Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why did Subhas and Sisir, as their plan of escape, decide to drive out in 'the most natural fashion through the main gate'?

Answer: They decided to drive out through the main gate in the most natural fashion to avoid raising any suspicion among the British police who were keeping a strict watch on Subhas's house.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What items were purchased for Subhas to be carried with him on his journey?

Answer: The items purchased for Subhas's journey were baggy shalwars, a black fez, a suitcase, bedroll, shirts, pillows, and calling cards printed with the false identity of 'Muhammad Ziauddin'.

(b) What was the real identity of Muhammad Ziauddin?

Answer: The real identity of Muhammad Ziauddin was Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose.

(c) Why do you think Subhas had to put on a false identity?

Answer: Subhas had to put on a false identity to deceive the British police watching his house, allowing him to travel secretly across India without being identified and arrested.

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) In which direction did Sisir drive after leaving Elgin Road?

Answer: Sisir drove towards Howrah Bridge, crossed it, and went beyond the city's precincts.

(b) Who was Ashok? How did he help in Subhas's escape?

Answer: Ashok was Subhas's brother (actually Sisir's brother, Ashok, who lived in Bararee near Dhanbad). He helped by giving Subhas (disguised as Muhammad Ziauddin) a room to rest during the day, preparing an early dinner, and keeping his escape secret.

(c) Describe Sisir's last sight of Subhas.

Answer: Sisir's last sight of Subhas was him mounting the railway overbridge at Gomoh station and walking across it with his usual majestic gait into the darkness toward the platform.

(d) Why did Sisir pick Subhas up in his car a little further from Ashok's house?

Answer: Sisir picked Subhas up a little further from Ashok's house to avoid any suspicion among the servants, making it seem as if Ziauddin was leaving for the station independently.
