Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Why do you think Jon thought that the turtle would be safe on his head?

Answer: Jon thought the turtle would be safe on his head because while working on the farm, putting the small turtle on his head kept it away from the heavy plough and from being accidentally crushed under his feet.

  • (b) Seeing a ship anchored near the shore Jon requested the captain to take him along.
  • (c) By twisting the cap, Jon could summon the east wind and the west wind.
  • (d) Jon desired to see the land because he had not set foot on land for a year and a day.

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did the fairyman offer Jon the wind-cap?

Answer: The fairyman offered Jon the wind-cap as a reward to thank him for his kindness in saving his life.

(b) What was the condition that was associated with the wind cap?

Answer: The condition was that no human hand would ever be able to take the wind-cap off Jon's head.

(c) Why did Jon become popular with the sailors?

Answer: Jon became popular because he could summon the winds (east, west, north, and south) by twisting his cap, providing the exact winds the sailors needed for sailing.

Activity 6

Write 'T' for true and 'F' for false statements in the given boxes. Give supporting statements for each of your answers:

(a) Jon never dreamt about the land. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "Jon could not stop dreaming of the land."

(b) The squall was called up because Jon was sleeping. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "One quiet afternoon, he lay fast asleep and fell to dreaming... It called up a squall from the clear sky..."

(c) The captain was angry with Jon. — [ T ]

Supporting Statement: "In anger, he grabbed him by the tail of his striped cap, twisted him thrice and flung him out to the sea."

(d) Jon did not return the wind-cap to the fairy man. — [ F ]

Supporting Statement: "He placed the wind cap under a stone where he knew the fairy man would find it."

Activity 7

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) Why did the sailors try to rip off the cap from Jon's head?

Answer: The sailors tried to rip off the cap because a terrible storm was destroying their ship, and they believed the storm was called up by Jon's cap.

(b) How did Jon divide his time between land and sea?

Answer: Jon divided his time by spending half the year on a ship at sea and the other half on the shore working on his farm.

(c) Do you think Jon was rightly called Captain Turtle? Give reasons for your answer.

Answer: Yes, Jon was rightly called Captain Turtle because he was equally comfortable, skilled, and at home both on the water and on the land, much like a turtle.

(d) Why did Jon want to return the wind cap to the fairy man?

Answer: Jon wanted to return the wind-cap because its magical power had caused a disastrous storm, put his fellow sailors' lives in danger, and brought him great trouble.

Activity 10 (a)

Write a paragraph in about eighty words describing your experience of the sea voyage on open sea with waves rising:

Last summer, I had the thrilling experience of going on a sea voyage on the open sea. As our ship sailed far from the coast, the water turned a deep blue, and massive waves began rising all around us. At first, the rocky movement of the boat made me feel quite scared and dizzy. However, watching the white foam of the churning waves and the endless horizon soon filled me with great excitement. It was an adventurous journey that I will always remember.
