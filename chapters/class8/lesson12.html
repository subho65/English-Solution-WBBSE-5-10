Class VIII English (Blossoms)

Lesson 12: Someone — Activity Answers

Activity 1

Tick the correct alternative:

  • (i) The door was — (b) small
  • (ii) The poet looked — (c) to left and right
  • (iii) The busy beetle was tap-tapping in the — (a) wall
  • (iv) The cricket was — (b) whistling

Activity 2

Complete the following sentences with information from the text:

  • (a) Someone came knocking at the poet's wee, small door.
  • (b) There was no stirring in the still, dark night.
  • (c) The poet heard the screech-owl’s call from the forest.
  • (d) The poet did not know who came knocking at all, at all, at all.

Activity 3

Answer the following question:

Who do you think came knocking at the poet's small door?

Answer: I think it was the wind or some mysterious spirit of the dark forest that came knocking at the poet's small door.

Activity 4

Fill in the following chart with information from the text:

Who Did What
(i) Someone came knocking at the small door
(ii) Beetle was tap-tapping in the wall
(iii) Owl screeched from the forest
(iv) Cricket whistled while the dewdrops fell

Activity 5

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did the poet do after he heard the knocking on the door?

Answer: After he heard the knocking, the poet listened, opened the door, and looked to the left and right to see if anyone was there.

(b) What was the night like?

Answer: The night was dark, completely still, and quiet, with dewdrops falling gently around the forest.

(c) Name the insect mentioned in the poem.

Answer: The beetle (and the cricket) are the insects mentioned in the poem.

(d) Why does the poet use the expression ‘at all’ thrice in the last line of the poem?

Answer: The poet uses the expression "at all" three times to strongly emphasize the absolute mystery and his complete ignorance about who had knocked on his door.

Activity 6 (b)

Underline the phrases in the following sentences and state what kind of phrases they are:

  • (i) He wanted to speak to his teacher. → Noun Phrase
  • (ii) At this moment it's raining hard. → Adverb Phrase
  • (iii) To do well in the competition is my aim. → Noun Phrase
  • (iv) They live in a house made of wood. → Adjective Phrase

Activity 6 (c)

Underline the clauses in the following sentences and state what kind of clauses they are:

  • (i) The child ran away as soon as she saw the strange man. → Adverb Clause
  • (ii) I saw an old woman who was carrying a child. → Adjective Clause
  • (iii) The dog follows his master wherever he goes. → Adverb Clause
  • (iv) We all thought that it would not rain today. → Noun Clause

Activity 6 (d)

Change the following sentences from Active to Passive Voice:

  • (i) India won the World Cup in cricket recently.
    Answer: The World Cup in cricket was won by India recently.
  • (ii) The teacher was teaching English.
    Answer: English was being taught by the teacher.
  • (iii) Rani is singing a beautiful song.
    Answer: A beautiful song is being sung by Rani.
  • (iv) The wind blew away the rooftops of the houses.
    Answer: The rooftops of the houses were blown away by the wind.

Activity 7

Make meaningful sentences of your own with the following words:

  • wee: The little sparrow carried wee bits of straw to build its nest.
  • stirring: There was absolutely no stirring in the quiet, dark forest at night.
  • busy: The busy ants worked hard all day to store food for winter.
  • dewdrops: In the morning, sparkling dewdrops glistened beautifully on the green grass.

Activity 8 (a)

Suppose you spent a moonlit night in a forest guest house. Write a paragraph in about eighty words on your experience. Mention the sounds that you heard there:

During my last winter vacation, I spent a magical, moonlit night in a quiet forest guest house in the Sunderbans. The forest was washed in pale silver light, and the towering trees cast long, mysterious shadows. From my open window, the night felt incredibly peaceful yet alive with nature. I heard the sharp, rhythmic whistling of crickets, the eerie screeching of an owl from the dark woods, and the gentle rustling of dry leaves in the breeze. It was an enchanting experience that filled me with absolute wonder.

Activity 8 (b)

Write a paragraph in about eighty words writing an autobiography of an owl enjoying the night:

I am an owl, the silent ruler of the night. While the rest of the world falls asleep, my day begins when the sun goes down. My large, sensitive eyes can see perfectly in the pitch darkness, and my soft wings allow me to fly silently through the trees. I love sitting on a high branch, enjoying the cool night air and watching the quiet movements below. When the moon shines bright, I glide through the forest openings and screech loudly to greet the night. The dark, mysterious forest is my peaceful home, and I enjoy every moment of the quiet night.
