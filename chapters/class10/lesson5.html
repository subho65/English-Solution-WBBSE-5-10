Exercise 9

Answer the following questions in complete sentences:

(a) What did Father find when he went back home years afterward?

Answer: When Father went back home years afterward, he found his brother had died and he couldn't find his sister.

(b) Where did Aunt Esther live?

Answer: Aunt Esther lived hundreds of miles inland.

(c) Why did Aunt Esther turn pale?

Answer: Aunt Esther turned pale because she recognized the runaway kite was patched with the very letter she had once written to her brother, bearing her parents' names—Philippa and Claude.

Exercise 10

Change the following sentences into questions, as directed:

  • (a) Shankha lives in Alipurduar. (Information question using ‘where’)
    Answer: Where does Shankha live?
  • (b) They have gone to a picnic. (Interrogative sentence using ‘where’)
    Answer: Where have they gone?
  • (c) I went to school yesterday. (Simple question using ‘did’)
    Answer: Did you go to school yesterday?
  • (d) Tia studies in class X. (Information question using ‘which’)
    Answer: Which class does Tia study in?

Exercise 11

Write an imaginary dialogue (within 100 words) between a bicycle repair shopkeeper and you after your tyre suddenly punctured on the way to school:

Me: Hello Kakababu! My bicycle tyre has suddenly punctured. Can you fix it quickly? I am getting late for school.
Shopkeeper: Hello! Yes, bring it here. Let me check where the leak is. Ah, I see a small nail stuck in the rear tyre.
Me: Oh dear! How long will it take to repair?
Shopkeeper: Don't worry, it will only take about five minutes. Sit down for a moment while I fix the tube and pump it up.
Me: Thank you so much! Here is the money. I will ride fast now.

Exercise 12

Write a story (within 100 words) about the clever crow and the fox using the hints on page 47:

A crow was sitting on a tree branch, holding a piece of meat in its beak. A hungry fox passing by saw the meat and wanted to grab it. The fox began praising the crow's beauty and asked him to sing. However, this crow was wise. He remembered the old story. He carefully put the meat under his feet and began to sing loudly. Seeing that his trick had failed and he had been fooled, the disappointed fox quietly left the spot. The clever crow happily enjoyed his meal.
