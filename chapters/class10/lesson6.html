Exercise 3

Answer the following questions within twenty-five words:

(a) During what time of the day does the poet wish to go down to the seas?

Answer: The poet wishes to go down to the seas at the break of a grey dawn when the morning mist is rising from the sea's face.

(b) What kind of day does the poet prefer for sailing?

Answer: The poet prefers a windy day with white clouds flying in the sky, flung spray, blown spume, and the sound of seagulls crying.

(c) What does the poet prefer to hear from a fellow rover?

Answer: The poet prefers to hear a merry yarn, which means a cheerful and funny story, from a laughing fellow-rover.

Exercise 5

Write a summary of the passage on page 51-52 about Tutankhamun (within 100 words):

Tutankhamun, the son of Akhenaten, was a young Egyptian king (Pharaoh) who ascended the throne at the age of ten and ruled between 1332-1323 BC. He undertook numerous significant building projects, constructing temples and monuments centered around Thebes and Karnak. Tutankhamun suffered from poor health throughout his life and required a cane to walk. After his death, his body was preserved as a mummy. In 1922, Englishman Howard Carter discovered his nearly intact tomb, sparking global interest in ancient Egypt.
